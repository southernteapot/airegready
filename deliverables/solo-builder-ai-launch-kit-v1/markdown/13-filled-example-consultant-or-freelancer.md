# Filled Example: Consultant Or Freelancer Using AI

Purpose: Show how a solo service provider can document AI use in client-facing work without overcomplicating the process.

## When To Use It

Use this example if you are a freelancer, consultant, strategist, designer, writer, marketer, automation builder, researcher, or productized-service seller using AI to support client work.

## How To Read It

This is fictional. It shows a practical record for AI-assisted drafting and summarizing. It does not replace client contract review, privacy review, or professional obligations.

## Example Project

Project: Northstar Studio Client Intake Workflow

Scenario: A solo brand strategist uses AI to summarize client intake forms, organize interview notes, and draft first-pass messaging outlines. The consultant reviews and edits everything before sending it to clients.

The workflow is internal drafting support. Clients see final edited deliverables, not raw AI output.

## Data Intake Concerns

| Concern | Example handling |
| --- | --- |
| Client confidential information | Client intake form says not to include passwords, trade secrets, non-public financials, regulated personal data, sensitive employee/customer data, or confidential third-party data unless separately agreed. |
| Contract or NDA limits | Consultant checks client terms before using AI on client-provided material. Unknown terms mean pause AI use for that material. |
| Pasted notes | Consultant pastes only the information needed to summarize scope and messaging. Raw recordings are not uploaded. |
| Vendor settings | Consultant keeps a note of vendor data-use settings and reviews them before using a new tool. |
| Client records | Final deliverables and approved notes are stored in the client project folder. Raw AI outputs are deleted when no longer needed for drafting. |

## Client Disclosure Draft

I may use AI tools to help organize intake notes, summarize themes, draft outlines, and prepare first-pass messaging options. I review and edit AI-assisted work before sending deliverables to you.

Please do not include passwords, API keys, payment information, sensitive personal information, regulated information, trade secrets, or confidential third-party data in intake materials unless we have specifically agreed on how that information should be handled.

AI tools support the drafting process, but they do not replace my review, judgment, or your final approval of messaging.

## Output Limitations

| Possible issue | Review step |
| --- | --- |
| AI invents a customer pain point | Check against intake form and interview notes. |
| AI overstates a product benefit | Rewrite as a narrower claim or mark as needs evidence. |
| AI creates generic positioning | Edit based on client-specific details and strategy. |
| AI suggests legal, regulated, or industry-specific claim language | Remove from draft and flag for client review with qualified counsel or appropriate advisor if needed. |
| AI includes confidential details in a draft section | Remove detail, check source, and update data instructions. |

## Review Carefully If

- A client asks you to process sensitive, regulated, employee, customer, or confidential third-party data.
- The AI is used for legal, medical, financial, employment, hiring, credit, housing, insurance, or other professional or high-stakes advice.
- Client contracts, platform terms, confidentiality promises, or data-processing rules limit what tools can process client material.
- Raw AI output is sent to clients without review.

Short boundary: This example is educational information, not legal advice. It shows one practical documentation style, not a legal conclusion about any specific client workflow.
