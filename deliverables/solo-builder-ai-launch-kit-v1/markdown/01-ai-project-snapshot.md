# AI Project Snapshot

Purpose: Capture the basic facts about your AI project so the launch does not live only in your head, codebase, prompts, support inbox, or sales copy.

## When To Use It

Use this worksheet when you are planning, prototyping, launching, selling, demoing, advertising, changing, or delivering an AI-enabled app, website, automation, consulting workflow, digital product, micro-SaaS, paid template, or client deliverable.

Use it again whenever the AI feature, model, vendor, data flow, public claims, human review process, or user-facing behavior changes.

## What This Produces

You should finish with a one-page record of:

- what the project does
- where AI is involved
- who uses or relies on the output
- what data touches the AI workflow
- what claims users may see
- what risks or unknowns need review
- when the next review should happen

## How To Fill It Out

Write short, plain answers. If you do not know something yet, write Unknown, Needs review, or Not applicable. The point is to make gaps visible, not to produce a perfect document.

## Review Carefully If

- You are making accuracy, earnings, safety, professional-advice, automation, or business-outcome claims.
- The AI touches sensitive personal data, client confidential information, children or minors, credentials, payment data, or regulated subject matter.
- The AI affects real-world decisions about people.
- Users may rely on outputs without human review.
- You changed vendors, models, prompts, data sources, public copy, or support processes since the last review.

Short boundary: This worksheet is educational information, not legal advice. It helps you document your AI project and spot questions to review. Review specific legal, privacy, contractual, or regulatory obligations with qualified counsel or the appropriate advisor.
