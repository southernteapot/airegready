# 30-Minute Setup Guide

Purpose: Create the shortest useful version of the Solo Builder AI Launch Kit when you need a working launch record today.

## When To Use It

Use this when you are close to launching, publishing, demoing, selling, advertising, onboarding users, or sending AI-assisted client work and need a first record before you go further.

This is the minimum viable version, not the full review path.

## How To Use It

Set a 30-minute timer. Write rough but honest answers. Use Unknown, Needs review, or Not applicable where needed. Do not stop to polish language unless it will be user-facing.

If a serious red flag appears, do not force a launch decision just because the timer is running. Mark the blocker and pause or narrow the launch.

## 0-5 Minutes: Project Snapshot

Open `01-ai-project-snapshot.md` and complete only these fields:

- project name
- public offer name
- owner
- date
- stage
- what the project does
- who uses it
- who pays for it
- where AI is used
- AI model/vendor/tool
- user data involved
- outputs produced
- human review points
- main risks or unknowns
- next review date

Minimum useful answer:

> [PROJECT] uses AI to [TASK] for [USER]. It uses [DATA] to produce [OUTPUT]. [PERSON] reviews [OUTPUT] before [USE]. Main unknowns: [UNKNOWN].

## 5-10 Minutes: Claims

Open `02-ai-claims-checklist.md` and find the 3-5 claims most likely to matter.

Look at:

- homepage or landing page hero copy
- product description
- checkout or marketplace copy
- ads or social posts
- sales emails or client proposal language
- onboarding and FAQ copy

For each claim, ask:

- Is it specific?
- Is it true today?
- Can I support it?
- Could a user hear it as a guarantee?
- Does it imply legal, financial, medical, professional, safety, compliance, accuracy, automation, or business-outcome advice?

Minimum useful answer:

Rewrite or remove the one claim that would create the most confusion if a buyer repeated it back to you.

## 10-15 Minutes: Data

Open `03-user-data-and-privacy-intake.md` and list:

- what users type, paste, upload, or connect
- what you paste into AI tools
- what goes to third-party AI vendors
- what prompts, logs, files, or outputs are saved
- what sensitive data users should avoid submitting
- whether users are told AI is involved

Minimum useful answer:

> This AI workflow uses [DATA] to produce [OUTPUT]. Users should not submit [DATA TO AVOID]. Data is sent to [VENDOR/TOOL] and stored in [LOCATION OR UNKNOWN].

## 15-20 Minutes: Red Flags

Open `05-red-flag-use-case-triage.md` and mark Yes, No, or Unsure for:

- health or medical
- mental health or crisis support
- legal
- financial, tax, credit, or insurance
- employment, HR, hiring, education, or housing
- children or minors
- biometrics, identity, surveillance, or fraud
- sensitive personal data
- client confidential or contract-restricted data
- scraping, third-party data, IP, or likeness
- automated decisions with real-world consequences

Minimum useful answer:

If anything is Yes or Unsure, write one next step: pause, narrow scope, add human review, get qualified advice, check platform/client terms, or document why the issue does not apply.

## 20-25 Minutes: Disclosure And FAQ

Open `04-ai-disclosure-and-disclaimer-builder.md` and draft three short notes:

1. AI-use disclosure: This feature uses AI to [TASK].
2. Output limitation: AI output may be incomplete or incorrect, so review it before use.
3. Problem reporting: Contact [EMAIL OR FORM] if something looks wrong.

Then open `08-customer-faq-template.md` and answer these three questions:

- Do you use AI?
- What data does the AI process?
- Can I rely on the output?

Minimum useful answer:

Put the disclosure where users will see it before relying on output. If no public placement exists yet, record where it needs to go.

## 25-30 Minutes: Launch Decision And Change Log

Open `07-pre-launch-ai-risk-checklist.md` and choose one final decision:

- Ready to launch as scoped
- Launch only after listed fixes
- Narrow scope before launch
- Private beta or limited test only
- Pause and get qualified help
- Do not launch in current form

Then open `10-ai-change-log.md` and add the launch baseline:

| Date | Change type | What changed | Why it changed | Next review |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [DATE] | Launch baseline | First version of AI project reviewed with Solo Builder AI Launch Kit. | Prepare before public or paid launch. | [DATE] |

Minimum useful answer:

Write the decision, the owner, the blockers, and the next review date.

## What Good Enough Looks Like

A good 30-minute version makes these things visible:

- what the AI does
- what data it touches
- what claims you are making
- what users should know
- what red flags might exist
- what needs to be fixed before launch
- what changed or needs review next

It does not need to sound polished. It needs to be specific enough that future you can understand what you decided.

## Review Carefully If

- You marked any red flag as Yes or Unsure.
- You are launching a paid offer before finishing the full review path.
- You do not yet have a user-facing disclosure or report-a-problem path.
- You found a claim that sounds like a guarantee, compliance promise, professional advice, accuracy promise, automation promise, or certain business outcome.
- You do not know what data is sent to a vendor or stored in logs.

Short boundary: This worksheet is educational information, not legal advice. It helps you create a first practical record and spot questions to review. Review specific legal, privacy, contractual, security, platform, or regulatory obligations with qualified counsel or the appropriate advisor.
