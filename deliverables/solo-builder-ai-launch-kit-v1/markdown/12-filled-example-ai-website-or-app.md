# Filled Example: AI Website Or App

Purpose: Show how the Solo Builder AI Launch Kit can look for a realistic AI-powered website or app.

## When To Use It

Use this example when your project is a website, no-code app, lightweight SaaS feature, wrapper, or user-facing AI tool.

## How To Read It

This is fictional. Copy the level of detail, not the exact facts. Your project may need different disclosures, review steps, or qualified help.

## Example Project

Project: LaunchPad Copy Lab

Scenario: A solo builder launches a paid web app that helps small creators draft first-pass landing-page copy from a short intake form.

The app is not intended to provide legal, financial, medical, tax, or professional advice. It drafts marketing copy ideas that users review and edit before publishing.

## User Data And Privacy Intake Excerpt

| Question | Example answer |
| --- | --- |
| What data is collected directly from users? | Email, account login, product name, audience, offer details, website copy pasted into the form, generated outputs. |
| What data is pasted into prompts? | Product details and requested tone. Users are instructed not to submit private, sensitive, confidential, regulated, credential, payment, or client-only information. |
| What data is sent to third-party AI vendors? | Intake form fields and copy-generation instructions. |
| Are logs retained? | App stores generated copy and intake history for the user's account until deleted. Vendor prompt-log setting still needs final review. |

## Disclosure Draft

This app uses AI to draft first-pass marketing copy from the product details you provide. The output may be incomplete, inaccurate, generic, or unsuitable for your specific offer. Review and edit all copy before publishing it.

Do not submit private, sensitive, confidential, regulated, credential, payment, or client-only information unless you are sure you have permission and the data is appropriate for this workflow.

This tool does not provide legal, financial, tax, or professional advice, and it does not promise business results.

To report incorrect, confusing, or unexpected output, contact [your support email or contact form] with the prompt, output, and a short description of the issue.

## Red Flag Triage Excerpt

| Category | Applies? | Notes | Next step |

| Sensitive personal data | Possible | Users could paste customer testimonials, client notes, or private business details. | Add do-not-submit instruction and review vendor settings. |
| Automated decisions | No | Tool drafts copy only. Users decide what to publish. | Document user review in disclosure and FAQ. |

Triage decision: Yellow. Continue with beta after vendor setting check, do-not-submit warning, and FAQ update.

## Customer FAQ Excerpt

### Do you use AI?

Yes. LaunchPad Copy Lab uses AI to draft landing-page copy ideas from the product details you provide.

### Can I publish the output as-is?

You should review and edit the output before publishing it. AI can miss context, invent details, or suggest claims that do not fit your product.

### What data does the AI process?

It processes the product details and tone instructions you enter. Do not submit private customer data, client confidential material, regulated information, passwords, API keys, payment details, or facts you cannot support.

### What should I not use this for?

Do not use it to create legal terms, professional advice, regulated claims, guaranteed earnings claims, or copy for products you cannot accurately describe or support.

## Review Carefully If

- The app starts making stronger conversion, revenue, compliance, accuracy, or professional-advice claims.
- Users can upload client files, customer lists, or sensitive data.
- The AI starts publishing copy automatically without user review.

Short boundary: This example is educational information, not legal advice. It shows one practical documentation style, not a legal conclusion about any specific product.
