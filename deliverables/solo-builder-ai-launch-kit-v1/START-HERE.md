# Start Here - Solo Builder AI Launch Kit v1

Thank you for using the Solo Builder AI Launch Kit.

This packet gives solo builders editable starting documents for documenting an AI-enabled offer before launch. It is built for AI apps, websites, automations, consulting workflows, client deliverables, digital products, productized services, and micro-SaaS tools.

## What is inside

The packet includes 14 documents in DOCX, PDF, and Markdown formats:

1. AI Project Snapshot
2. AI Claims Checklist
3. User Data and Privacy Intake
4. AI Disclosure and Disclaimer Builder
5. Red Flag Use Case Triage
6. Vendor / Model Dependency Note
7. Pre-Launch AI Risk Checklist
8. Customer FAQ Template
9. Incident / Complaint Mini-Log
10. AI Change Log
11. 30-Minute Setup Guide
12. Filled Example: AI Website or App
13. Filled Example: Consultant or Freelancer Using AI
14. Filled Example: Digital Product, Automation, or Micro-SaaS

It also includes starter spreadsheets (`spreadsheets/` folder): CSV versions of the Incident / Complaint Mini-Log and the AI Change Log that you can import into Excel, Google Sheets, or Airtable.

## Recommended first pass

1. Start with the 30-Minute Setup Guide if you need a quick first record.
2. Fill out the AI Project Snapshot so the project is clear.
3. Review public claims with the AI Claims Checklist.
4. Map user, customer, client, and vendor data in the User Data and Privacy Intake.
5. Run the Red Flag Use Case Triage before launch.
6. Record vendor/model dependencies and known limitations.
7. Draft user-facing disclosure and FAQ language.
8. Use the Pre-Launch AI Risk Checklist to choose a launch decision.
9. Keep the Incident / Complaint Mini-Log and AI Change Log active after launch.

## File formats

- `docx/` contains editable Word-compatible documents.
- `pdf/` contains PDF exports for easy review or circulation.
- `markdown/` contains plain-text source versions as a bonus/reference format.
- `spreadsheets/` contains CSV starter files for the incident/complaint mini-log and the AI change log. Import them into Excel, Google Sheets, or Airtable to keep these logs as living spreadsheets.

Most buyers should start with the DOCX files.

## Support placeholders

Customer-facing templates use placeholders such as `[your support email or contact form]`. Replace these with your own support email, support contact, contact form, or company support address before using the text publicly.

## Important boundary

These materials are educational templates and practical documentation starting points only. They are not legal advice, do not create an attorney-client relationship, do not make an AI project compliant, do not approve a use case, do not guarantee safety, and do not replace qualified legal, privacy, security, HR, procurement, platform, or sector-specific review.

Use them to document your project, spot obvious gaps, ask better questions, and prepare before launch. Review specific obligations with qualified counsel or the appropriate advisor.
