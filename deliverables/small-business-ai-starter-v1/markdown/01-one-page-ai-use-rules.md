## The five rules

1. **A human is always responsible.** AI can help draft, summarize, brainstorm, and analyze. It does not own the final answer.
2. **Confidential stays confidential.** Do not paste private company, customer, employee, financial, health, contract, or source-code information into unapproved AI tools.
3. **Check the output.** AI can sound confident while being wrong. Verify facts, citations, names, numbers, legal claims, and code before relying on the result.
4. **Be honest about AI use.** Disclose AI assistance when the recipient, customer, client, court, regulator, employer, or audience would reasonably care.
5. **Ask before risky uses.** If AI is being used for decisions about people, sensitive data, customer-facing advice, legal/medical/financial topics, or automated workflows, pause and ask for review.

---

## What approved AI tools may be used for

Employees may use approved AI tools to:

- brainstorm ideas and outlines
- summarize public or approved internal materials
- draft and edit emails, memos, and internal documents
- help with code, if the code is reviewed and tested
- translate or simplify non-sensitive text
- create first drafts of marketing copy, FAQs, checklists, or internal resources

A person must review the output before it is sent, published, shipped, or relied on.

---

## What AI tools may not be used for without approval

Do not use AI tools to:

- paste customer, employee, health, financial, biometric, children’s, source-code, contract, or confidential data into unapproved tools
- make final hiring, firing, pay, promotion, discipline, lending, housing, healthcare, education, insurance, or legal-service decisions
- generate customer-facing legal, medical, financial, or professional advice without qualified human review
- create deepfakes, voice clones, synthetic likenesses, impersonation, or deceptive content
- install unapproved AI tools or connect AI tools to company systems
- hide AI use when disclosure is required or expected

## Data traffic light

**Green — generally okay in approved tools**

- public information
- generic industry facts
- non-sensitive brainstorming prompts

**Yellow — use only in tools approved for internal data**

- internal memos
- draft strategies
- non-sensitive business documents
- anonymized or aggregated information

**Red — do not enter unless specifically approved for that data type**

- customer or employee personal data
- health, financial, biometric, children’s, or government ID data
- contracts, pricing, source code, trade secrets, or NDA material
- anything regulated, privileged, confidential, or highly sensitive

When in doubt: **do not paste it. Ask first.**
