---

## When to list a tool

List a tool if any of these are true:

- employees use it for work
- it produces text, code, images, audio, video, recommendations, scores, or decisions
- it has AI features turned on, even if AI is not the main product
- it connects to company systems such as email, files, CRM, code, HR, support, or finance systems
- a team member uses a personal AI account for company work

If you are not sure, list it and mark the status as **Needs review**.

## Risk flags

Mark **Needs review** if any answer below is yes:

- Does the tool receive customer, employee, health, financial, biometric, children’s, source-code, contract, or confidential data?
- Is the output shown directly to customers or the public?
- Does the tool make or materially influence decisions about hiring, employment, lending, housing, healthcare, education, insurance, benefits, legal rights, or other important services?
- Does the tool connect to internal systems or automate actions?
- Does the vendor use your inputs to train models by default?
- Is the vendor’s data retention unclear?
- Is there no obvious owner inside the company?

## Monthly/quarterly review checklist

- [ ] Add any new tools employees started using.
- [ ] Remove or mark retired tools no longer used.
- [ ] Confirm owners are still correct.
- [ ] Confirm data entered into each tool has not changed.
- [ ] Confirm customer-facing or people-decision use has not started without review.
- [ ] Follow up on every **Needs review** item.
- [ ] Save the review date.
