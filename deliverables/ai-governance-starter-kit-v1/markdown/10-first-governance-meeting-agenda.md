# First AI Governance Meeting Agenda

**Company:** [COMPANY NAME]
**Meeting owner:** [AI GOVERNANCE LEAD]
**Date:** ______________________________
**Length:** 30 minutes default / 60 minutes if needed

> **How to use.** Use this agenda for the first meeting after drafting the inventory and AI use rules. The goal is not to solve every AI issue. The goal is to agree on owners, rules, review triggers, and next actions.

---

## Who should attend

Invite only the people needed for a practical first pass:

- AI governance owner or operations lead
- Founder, executive sponsor, or department lead
- IT/security or systems owner if available
- Privacy/legal/compliance contact if available
- Representatives from teams already using AI heavily

For a very small team, two or three people is enough.

---

## Pre-work

Send these at least one day before the meeting:

- Draft AI Use Inventory
- Draft AI Acceptable Use Policy
- Green / Yellow / Red AI Use Rules
- List of higher-risk workflows or open questions
- Proposed owner for the update tracker

Ask attendees to come prepared with:

- AI tools their team uses
- Any sensitive-data concerns
- Any customer-facing AI use
- Any new tool requests
- Any rule that seems unclear or impractical

## 30-minute agenda

| Time | Topic | Goal |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0:00-0:05 | Why we are doing this | Align on practical purpose, not bureaucracy |
| 0:05-0:12 | Inventory review | Confirm known tools, owners, and gaps |
| 0:12-0:18 | Rules review | Confirm approved tools, data rules, and prohibited uses |
| 0:18-0:23 | Review triggers | Decide what needs intake before use |
| 0:23-0:27 | Open risks | Identify top issues needing legal/privacy/security/leadership review |
| 0:27-0:30 | Next actions | Assign owners and dates |

---

## 60-minute agenda

| Time | Topic | Goal |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0:00-0:05 | Purpose and boundaries | Confirm this is educational/internal governance work, not legal advice |
| 0:05-0:15 | Inventory walk-through | Review each known AI tool and owner |
| 0:15-0:25 | Data rules | Confirm what data is allowed, restricted, and prohibited |
| 0:25-0:35 | Higher-risk workflows | Flag customer-facing, regulated, or decision-related use |
| 0:35-0:43 | New tool review path | Confirm mini-checklist vs full intake triggers |
| 0:43-0:50 | Staff rollout | Decide who sends the rollout message and when |
| 0:50-0:56 | Update rhythm | Assign update tracker owner and review cadence |
| 0:56-1:00 | Decisions and next actions | Confirm owners, dates, and open review items |

---

## Decisions to make in the first meeting

- [ ] Who owns the AI Use Inventory?
- [ ] Who owns the AI Acceptable Use Policy?
- [ ] Who can approve low-risk AI tool requests?
- [ ] What triggers full AI Risk Intake?
- [ ] Which tools are approved now?
- [ ] Which tools are paused or under review?
- [ ] What data is prohibited in AI tools without approval?
- [ ] Who sends the rollout message?
- [ ] When is the next review meeting?

## Facilitator notes

- Keep the meeting practical. If a topic becomes legal or technical, assign a follow-up instead of guessing.
- Do not let perfect classification block basic visibility.
- Record conditions and open questions in the inventory or update tracker.
- End with owners and dates.
