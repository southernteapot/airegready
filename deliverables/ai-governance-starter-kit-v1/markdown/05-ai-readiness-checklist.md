> **How to use.** Use this checklist to turn the starter kit into a practical first-pass governance file. Do not wait for perfection. The goal is to make AI use visible, assign ownership, set basic rules, and identify the higher-risk items that need deeper review.

---

## Status key

Use the same status labels across the checklist:

- **Not started** - no owner or draft exists
- **Drafted** - first draft exists but has not been reviewed
- **In use** - adopted for the team and available to users
- **Needs review** - requires legal, privacy, security, procurement, or leadership review
- **Done enough for first pass** - complete enough to operate while improving over time

## 6. Higher-risk workflows

Flag any workflow that touches one or more of the following:

- Employment, hiring, promotion, discipline, or termination
- Credit, lending, insurance, pricing, eligibility, or benefits
- Housing, education, healthcare, legal services, or government services
- Children, students, patients, employees, applicants, or vulnerable people
- Customer-facing chatbot or automated communication
- Sensitive personal data, biometric data, health data, financial data, or regulated records
- Safety, cybersecurity, production systems, or autonomous tool use

## 8. First-pass completion check

Mark the packet **done enough for first pass** when all of these are true:

- [ ] Active AI tools are listed in the inventory
- [ ] Each active AI tool has an owner
- [ ] Approved and unapproved tools are distinguishable
- [ ] Staff-facing AI use rules exist
- [ ] Sensitive-data rules are written down
- [ ] New AI tool requests have an intake path
- [ ] Higher-risk workflows are flagged for review
- [ ] An update tracker is started
- [ ] Staff know where to ask AI use questions
