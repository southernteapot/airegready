## First 10 LinkedIn Posts

1. Founders do not need a 90-page AI governance manual to start. They need a list of tools, a few written rules, a risk intake path, and a way to track changes. I put together a practical AI governance checklist here: https://airegready.com/ai-governance-checklist

2. If your business uses ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot, or AI features inside vendor tools, the first question is simple: what are people allowed to put into them? Start with employee AI use rules before the tool list gets messy: https://airegready.com/chatgpt-policy-for-employees

3. A small business AI policy should answer four questions: which tools are approved, what data is off-limits, when human review is required, and who owns updates. More here: https://airegready.com/ai-policy-template-small-business

4. Before writing an AI policy, make an inventory. List every AI tool, owner, workflow, data category, approval status, and review date. If you cannot see the AI stack, you cannot govern it. Starter guide: https://airegready.com/ai-use-inventory-template

5. Not every AI workflow deserves the same review. Internal brainstorming is different from tools that affect customers, employees, pricing, eligibility, or sensitive data. First-pass risk assessment guide: https://airegready.com/ai-risk-assessment-template

6. If you are starting a business with AI, create a lightweight governance file before customers, contractors, or staff start making their own rules. Free checklist: https://airegready.com/checklist

7. The most useful AI policy for a new business is the one people can actually follow. Keep it short, name approved tools, block sensitive data, require human review for important outputs, and update it when workflows change. https://airegready.com/ai-policy-template-small-business

8. A quick AI inventory can reveal more than a long strategy session: personal accounts, free trials, AI inside existing vendors, customer data in prompts, and workflows nobody formally approved. https://airegready.com/ai-use-inventory-template

9. My practical test for first-pass AI risk: who is affected, what data is used, what decision is supported, what could go wrong, and who reviews the output before it matters? https://airegready.com/ai-risk-assessment-template

10. I built the AI Governance Starter Kit for the point where AI use is already happening but the written process is missing: inventory, policy, intake, risk tiering, risk register, rollout, and update tracking in one editable packet. https://airegready.com/catalog/ai-governance-starter-kit
