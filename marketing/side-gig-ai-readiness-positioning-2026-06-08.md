# Side-Gig AI Readiness Positioning - 2026-06-08

Purpose: add a more specific AIRegReady buyer lane for people using AI to make side income before paid advertising begins. This should sit beside the founder/new-business/small-team lane, not replace it.

## Working Buyer

The best first advertised buyer may be:

> A solo person using AI to make a little money with a side gig who wants to avoid obvious trouble before selling, publishing, advertising, or taking clients.

Examples:

- Gumroad digital-product seller
- Etsy / Shopify / marketplace seller using AI-assisted content or designs
- freelancer using AI in client work
- consultant building AI-assisted deliverables
- creator selling templates, guides, prompts, courses, or worksheets
- indie builder making a small app, automation, or AI wrapper
- newsletter, blog, or social creator using AI for paid content
- person testing a weekend business before forming a real company

## Why This Buyer Is Promising

- The anxiety is concrete: "I want to make money, but I do not want to get in trouble."
- The buyer does not need enterprise governance language.
- The buyer may pay for a low-cost checklist/kit before paying for formal legal or compliance work.
- The product can be advertised around a practical pre-launch moment: before the first sale, first client, first ad, or first public product page.
- The existing Starter Kit can remain the more businesslike offer for founders and teams.

## Buyer Questions

Lead with these:

- Can I sell something AI helped me make?
- What should I disclose?
- What should I not paste into ChatGPT or Claude?
- What should I check before I run ads?
- What if I use AI for client work?
- How do I avoid making claims I cannot back up?
- What records should I keep if a platform, client, customer, or lawyer asks questions?
- When is a side-gig AI workflow too risky to handle casually?

Avoid making the buyer feel like they need a compliance department.

## Offer Concept

Working name: **Side-Gig AI Readiness Kit**

Short description:

> A practical pre-launch file for people using AI to build side income. Document what you used, what you checked, what you disclose, and what you should avoid before you sell, publish, advertise, or take clients.

Possible price test:

- $9 for the most accessible version
- $19 if delivered as polished DOCX/PDF/Markdown with strong examples and launch checklist

Do not advertise until:

- the product has polished DOCX/PDF/Markdown exports
- the ZIP is QA'd
- Gumroad title, description, support copy, refund/update notes, and legal notice are written
- the public catalog/product page has honest availability and Product/Offer metadata
- at least one landing page or article speaks directly to side-gig buyers

## Ad Angles To Test

### 1. Before you sell the AI thing

For people about to publish a Gumroad product, course, template, app, or client offer.

Message:

> Before you sell something AI helped you make, write down what you used, what you checked, what you disclose, and what you should avoid.

### 2. Keep AI out of the wrong data

For freelancers and consultants handling client work.

Message:

> If you use AI in client work, know what not to paste into AI tools and keep a record of how outputs are reviewed before delivery.

### 3. Claims before ads

For side-gig sellers preparing product pages or paid ads.

Message:

> AI can write confident sales copy. You still need to check claims before you publish, sell, or run ads.

### 4. Side income without enterprise theater

For solo builders who find AI governance language overwhelming.

Message:

> You do not need an enterprise AI governance program for a side gig. You do need a short file for tools, data rules, claims, disclosure, risk triage, and launch readiness.

## First 10 Posts

1. If you are using AI to make side money, the first question is not "do I need a compliance program?" It is "what should I write down before I sell this?" Start with tools used, data rules, human review, claims, disclosure, and launch notes.

2. A Gumroad product made with AI still needs human review. Check the claims, sources, examples, customer promises, platform rules, and whether buyers would expect to know AI helped create it.

3. Freelancers using AI in client work need one plain rule: do not paste client data into AI tools unless the client, contract, tool, and workflow allow that use.

4. The side-gig AI mistake I would avoid: selling AI-assisted advice in legal, financial, medical, employment, housing, insurance, education, or similar areas without qualified review.

5. AI can help you draft a product page. It cannot prove your claims. Before ads, list every claim and the evidence behind it.

6. If your side gig uses AI images, voices, logos, likenesses, or style references, pause before publishing. Platform rules, consent, disclosure, and IP questions matter.

7. You do not need a 90-page governance manual for a weekend business. You need a visible file: tools, workflows, data rules, claims checked, disclosure decisions, and open questions.

8. The best AI side-gig launch checklist is not fancy. Did you use sensitive data? Did you check claims? Did a human review outputs? Do platform rules require labels? Are refunds/support clear?

9. If you use ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or Copilot to help create client deliverables, keep a basic record of what AI helped with and what you reviewed before delivery.

10. Side-gig AI readiness is not about fear. It is about being able to answer: what did I use, what did I check, what did I disclose, and what did I decide not to do?

## Product Relationship

- **Side-Gig AI Readiness Kit:** best entry offer for solo side-income builders before advertising.
- **AI Governance Starter Kit:** best next step for people turning the side gig into a real business, adding contractors, taking more clients, or needing a broader operating file.
- **Vendor AI Review Packet:** best follow-on for businesses choosing AI vendors or SaaS AI features.

## Public Copy Guardrails

Use:

- "avoid obvious mistakes"
- "organize a first AI side-gig file"
- "before you sell, publish, advertise, or take clients"
- "document what you used and checked"
- "educational templates only"
- "route serious legal, privacy, security, tax, platform, or client questions to qualified reviewers"

Avoid:

- "stay compliant"
- "avoid legal trouble" as a guaranteed outcome
- "protect yourself legally"
- "AI law kit"
- "lawyer-approved"
- "regulator-ready"
- "everything you need"
