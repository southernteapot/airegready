# UK AI Policy — Readiness Overview

**UK landscape:**

- **UK GDPR** and **Data Protection Act 2018** — the backbone of UK AI compliance for personal data
- **ICO guidance on AI** — including Guidance on AI and data protection, Explaining Decisions Made with AI
- **Government AI Regulation White Paper** and response — pro-innovation, principles-based, sector-led
- **Sector regulators** — FCA, MHRA, Ofcom, Ofqual, CMA, ICO — each publishing sector-specific AI guidance
- **Online Safety Act** — for online platforms, addresses AI-generated harmful content
- **Competition (CMA) AI merger and market inquiries** — watching foundation model concentration
- **Draft AI (Regulation) Bill** (private members' bill) — monitoring
- **UK AI Safety Institute** — evaluating frontier models
- **AI Standards Hub** — industry-standard tracking

> **How to use this kit.** For businesses operating in the UK or serving UK customers. The UK approach differs from the EU's comprehensive AI Act — it's sector-led and principles-based. Pair with the EU AI Act High-Risk Kit (if you're also in the EU), the GDPR ↔ EU AI Act Crosswalk (UK GDPR is very similar to EU GDPR), and the AI Governance Framework.

## 1. Why UK matters

- 6th largest economy; significant AI ecosystem
- Home to major regulators (FCA, ICO) with global influence
- Strong enforcement under UK GDPR (ICO has been active)
- Post-Brexit divergence from EU rules creates friction and opportunities
- UK AI Safety Institute is a global reference for frontier-model evaluation

## 2. The UK approach — pro-innovation and principles-based

Rather than a single horizontal AI law, the UK has chosen a principles-based, sector-led approach. Core principles:

1. **Safety, security, and robustness**
2. **Appropriate transparency and explainability**
3. **Fairness**
4. **Accountability and governance**
5. **Contestability and redress**

Each sector regulator applies these principles within their remit.

## 3. UK GDPR and AI

- Same framework as EU GDPR with UK-specific supervisory authority (ICO)
- ICO guidance on AI is detailed and practical
- DPIAs expected for high-risk AI processing
- **"Explaining decisions made with AI"** ICO / Alan Turing Institute guidance is the UK reference for explainability practice
- Automated decision-making rights under UK GDPR Art 22
- International transfers: UK IDTA or UK Addendum to SCCs

## 4. ICO AI guidance — practical points

- **Lawful basis**: each AI processing activity must have a lawful basis, documented per-purpose
- **Transparency**: data subjects should receive AI-appropriate privacy information
- **Fairness**: AI outputs should not produce unlawful discrimination; document fairness testing
- **Accuracy**: AI must maintain appropriate accuracy for its purpose
- **Accountability**: demonstrable via policies, DPIAs, records, and technical controls
- **Security**: model theft, inversion, evasion, and adversarial attacks included in risk analysis

## 5. Sector regulators — highlights

### FCA (financial services)

- AI governance expectations aligned with senior manager regime
- Consumer Duty considerations for AI decisions affecting consumers
- Model risk management; analogous to SR 11-7 mindset
- Explainability in consequential decisions

### MHRA (medical devices)

- Software as a Medical Device framework applies
- Separate UK conformity assessment post-Brexit (UKCA marking, with ongoing recognition of CE for some cases)
- Post-market surveillance for AI-enabled devices

### Ofcom (communications / Online Safety Act)

- Online Safety Act obligations include addressing AI-generated illegal content and harms to children
- Duty-of-care regime
- Risk assessments and content moderation

### Ofqual (qualifications and exams)

- AI in assessment and grading — regulated in education context

### CMA (competition)

- Foundation-model concentration, market inquiries
- Merger scrutiny for AI M&A

### MHRA / Care Quality Commission

- Healthcare AI and digital health apps

## 6. Online Safety Act

Applies to platforms that host user-generated content or allow UK users to encounter content. AI relevance:

- AI-generated illegal content (CSAM, terrorism)
- AI-generated content harmful to children
- AI detection / moderation practices
- Transparency reporting

Large services have categorised duties; Ofcom is the regulator. Heavy penalties.

## 7. Competition and merger control

CMA has taken an active interest in foundation-model partnerships and AI M&A. Expect scrutiny of:

- Partnerships between cloud providers and AI developers
- Exclusive arrangements
- Concentration concerns
- Price and quality impacts

## 8. UK AI Safety Institute

Operates voluntary evaluation arrangements with frontier model developers. Companies building or deploying frontier models in the UK should engage with AISI work.

## 9. Employment AI

UK employment law and Equality Act 2010 apply to AI in hiring and workplace. Key considerations:

- Disparate impact analysis
- Reasonable adjustments for disability
- Candidate notice (ICO guidance)
- Works council / consultation where applicable

## 10. Practical readiness path

1. **Map UK exposure** — users, employees, operations
2. **UK GDPR program** — records of processing, DPIAs, lawful basis documentation
3. **ICO alignment** — adopt the "Explaining decisions made with AI" approach for explainability
4. **Sector regulator alignment** — where you're regulated
5. **Transfer mechanisms** — UK IDTA or UK Addendum for transfers out of UK
6. **Accessibility** — disability discrimination under Equality Act
7. **Principles integration** — articulate the UK's five principles in your AI policy
8. **Online Safety Act compliance** — for platforms
9. **Monitor Bill / regulation evolution** — UK position is evolving

## 11. Common traps

- Treating UK GDPR as identical to EU GDPR in all respects (mostly, but not entirely)
- Using EU SCCs without the UK IDTA / Addendum for transfers touching UK data
- Assuming sectoral regulator guidance is optional (it's often de facto mandatory)
- Missing Online Safety Act obligations for platforms
- Not engaging sector regulators early when launching AI in a regulated domain
