## Phase 1 — Scope

- [ ] **1.1** AI System Inventory includes every AI system used with students, families, staff, or institutional data
- [ ] **1.2** Each system tagged for applicable laws (FERPA, COPPA, state student privacy, CAIA, EU AI Act, Section 504/ADA)
- [ ] **1.3** Roles identified: institution, vendor, parent-purchased
- [ ] **1.4** Age groups identified (under 13 triggers COPPA; under 18 often triggers state laws)

## Phase 2 — FERPA

- [ ] **2.1** Vendor relationships meet "school official" exception criteria
- [ ] **2.2** Vendor use is limited to authorized educational purpose
- [ ] **2.3** Redisclosure prohibited in vendor contracts
- [ ] **2.4** Student/parent rights workflows: access, amendment, directory opt-out
- [ ] **2.5** Annual FERPA notice updated to cover AI uses
- [ ] **2.6** PII disclosure log where required

## Phase 3 — COPPA (if under 13)

- [ ] **3.1** Notice to parents about data collection and AI uses
- [ ] **3.2** Verifiable parental consent captured
- [ ] **3.3** Data minimization enforced
- [ ] **3.4** No behavioral advertising to children
- [ ] **3.5** Deletion on request
- [ ] **3.6** Safe Harbor or FTC compliance review

## Phase 4 — State student privacy

- [ ] **4.1** Data Privacy Agreement (DPA) in place with every ed-tech vendor
- [ ] **4.2** State-specific requirements mapped (NY 2-d, CA SOPIPA, CO, CT, IL, others)
- [ ] **4.3** Public registry of vendor agreements (where required)
- [ ] **4.4** Prohibition on ads and targeting enforced
- [ ] **4.5** Incident response aligned with state breach rules

## Phase 5 — Accessibility and accommodations

- [ ] **5.1** WCAG 2.1 AA compliance for AI interfaces
- [ ] **5.2** IEP / 504 accommodations honored in AI-mediated learning
- [ ] **5.3** Alternative access paths for students who cannot use AI tool
- [ ] **5.4** Proctoring AI has documented accommodation procedures
- [ ] **5.5** Assistive technology compatibility tested

## Phase 6 — Admissions and placement AI

- [ ] **6.1** Disparate-impact testing on admissions/placement AI
- [ ] **6.2** Human decision-maker involved in final decisions
- [ ] **6.3** Applicant notice about AI use in admissions
- [ ] **6.4** Appeal path for adverse admissions/placement decisions
- [ ] **6.5** OCR complaint handling awareness

## Phase 7 — Assessment and grading

- [ ] **7.1** AI-assisted grading is disclosed to students
- [ ] **7.2** Rubric transparency — students can understand the grading basis
- [ ] **7.3** Appeal to human review available
- [ ] **7.4** Due-process pathway for disciplinary AI (plagiarism detection, etc.)
- [ ] **7.5** Faculty/teacher oversight of grading outputs

## Phase 8 — Proctoring

- [ ] **8.1** Proctoring use justified in writing (necessity, alternatives considered)
- [ ] **8.2** Disparate-impact evaluation
- [ ] **8.3** Disability accommodation path
- [ ] **8.4** Student notice of proctoring and data collected
- [ ] **8.5** Data retention and deletion
- [ ] **8.6** False-positive remediation workflow

## Phase 9 — Student-facing AI (chatbots, tutors, counseling)

- [ ] **9.1** Clear disclosure to students and parents
- [ ] **9.2** Scope statements (what the AI does and does not do)
- [ ] **9.3** Safety guardrails (suicide/self-harm, CSAM, abuse)
- [ ] **9.4** Escalation to human staff for at-risk signals
- [ ] **9.5** Age-appropriate design per state laws (CA AADC, UK AADC)
- [ ] **9.6** Parental consent where required

## Phase 10 — Staff AI and acceptable use

- [ ] **10.1** AI Acceptable Use Policy for staff
- [ ] **10.2** Rules against uploading student records to public AI tools
- [ ] **10.3** Training on AI literacy for faculty / teachers
- [ ] **10.4** Training on AI literacy for students (where required)

## Phase 11 — Research

- [ ] **11.1** IRB review for AI studies involving students
- [ ] **11.2** FERPA research exception analysis documented
- [ ] **11.3** De-identification standards applied where used

## Phase 12 — Colorado AI Act (if in scope)

- [ ] **12.1** Impact assessment for education AI in consequential-decision context
- [ ] **12.2** Consumer notice before consequential decision (applies to student/family)
- [ ] **12.3** Adverse-decision explanation and appeal
- [ ] **12.4** Public statement on institutional website

## Phase 13 — EU AI Act (if EU exposure)

- [ ] **13.1** Education systems in Annex III high-risk categories inventoried
- [ ] **13.2** Technical documentation (for providers) / deployer obligations
- [ ] **13.3** FRIA for applicable deployers
- [ ] **13.4** Transparency obligations under Art 50 for student-facing systems

## Phase 14 — Records

- [ ] **14.1** DPAs, impact assessments, bias evaluations, accommodation requests, complaints logged
- [ ] **14.2** Retention aligned to FERPA (typically student record retention rules), state laws, and CAIA/EU AI Act
