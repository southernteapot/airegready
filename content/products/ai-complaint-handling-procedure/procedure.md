## 1. Scope

This procedure covers complaints and concerns about:

- AI-assisted decisions ("I was denied and I don't understand why")
- AI outputs that appear biased, discriminatory, or inaccurate
- AI behavior that surprised, offended, or harmed a user
- Failure to provide required disclosures
- Failure to honor a request (opt-out, correction, appeal, alternative process)
- Suspected prohibited AI uses
- Accessibility complaints involving AI

It excludes routine product bugs and service-quality issues that don't implicate AI governance. Those stay in normal support.

## 2. Complainant types

- **Consumers / end users**
- **Candidates / employees**
- **Patients / beneficiaries**
- **Customers (B2B)**
- **Third parties** (parents, advocates, journalists)
- **Employees of [COMPANY NAME]** (internal reporters)
- **Regulators** (follow separate regulatory inquiry procedure)

## 4. Intake requirements (minimum fields)

- Complainant identification (or anonymous)
- Contact preference for response
- Which AI system / product / feature
- When the issue occurred
- What happened (plain description)
- What the complainant wants as a remedy
- Any evidence (screenshots, transcripts, reference numbers)

## 5. Acknowledgment

Acknowledge receipt within:

- **1 business day** for allegations of discrimination, harm, or regulatory violation
- **3 business days** for other AI complaints
- **Within the statutory window** if a regulator-initiated inquiry applies

Acknowledgment includes:

- Reference number
- Name and role of the person assigned
- Estimated response time
- How to submit additional information

## 6. Triage

Within [5] business days of intake, the assigned investigator classifies the complaint:

| Type | Definition | Escalation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Tier 1 — Service** | Routine quality issue, no AI-governance implication | Handle in standard support |
| **Tier 2 — Policy** | AI behavior outside policy; needs correction / communication | AI Governance Lead notified |
| **Tier 3 — Rights / harm** | Alleged discrimination, rights impact, harm to individual | AI Review Committee + Legal |
| **Tier 4 — Pattern / systemic** | Multiple complaints suggesting systemic bias or safety issue | Executive Sponsor; potential incident |
| **Tier 5 — Regulatory** | Implicates a regulator-notification clock | Legal; activate Incident Response if applicable |

## 7. Investigation steps

- Reproduce the issue if possible
- Pull relevant logs, prompts, outputs, decision records
- Identify the AI system, version, data sources
- Review the decision or output under applicable policy, law, and ethical framework
- Consult with the system's business owner and technical owner
- Consult Legal for regulatory implications
- Consult Privacy if personal data involved

Target: substantive response within [30] days, or explain why more time is needed.

## 8. Remedies

Options (individual or combined):

- **Explanation** — plain-language description of what happened and why
- **Correction** — of personal data used by the system
- **Appeal** — human review of the AI-assisted decision
- **Alternative decision** — reconsideration without AI or with different inputs
- **Apology** — when an outcome was wrong or harmful
- **Compensation** — where warranted
- **System change** — retrain, retire, add guardrails, add human review
- **Policy change** — update procedures or governance
- **Industry notice** — notify other affected users if pattern identified

## 9. Colorado AI Act — specific requirements

For consumers subject to consequential decisions in Colorado:

- Provide the principal reasons for the decision
- Enable correction of personal data used by the system
- Provide a right to appeal with human review (where technically feasible)
- Respond consistent with statutory timelines

For patterns suggesting algorithmic discrimination:

- Escalate to 90-day AG notification track
- Coordinate with Legal on notification content and timing

## 10. NYC LL 144 — specific requirements

For candidate complaints about AEDT use:

- Confirm notice was delivered
- Confirm accommodation / alternative selection requests were processed
- Provide the bias audit summary link
- Route to Legal if pattern of complaints suggests non-compliance

## 11. EU AI Act — specific considerations

- Persons subject to decisions by high-risk AI systems have a right to an explanation (Art 86)
- Serious incident reporting (Art 73) applies — if the complaint reveals a reportable incident
- Coordination with provider and authorized representative

## 12. Communications

- Written responses are reviewed by Legal for Tier 3+ complaints
- Complaint responses do not admit legal liability unless approved by Legal
- Empathetic tone; don't minimize
- Plain language; don't bury the answer in disclaimers

## 13. Documentation

For every complaint, retain:

- Intake record
- Investigation notes and evidence
- Correspondence with complainant
- Decision and rationale
- Remedy applied
- Follow-up actions and due dates
- Closure confirmation

Retention: at least [3-7 years] or longer per applicable law.

## 14. Trend analysis

- Monthly: category and severity counts; close rates; notable findings
- Quarterly: theme analysis; recurring failure modes; systemic-risk escalation
- Annual: complaint posture in the AI Board Report; updates to policies and training

## 15. Training

- Customer support team trained on AI complaint recognition and routing
- Investigators trained on AI governance, applicable laws, and investigation technique
- Legal and Privacy informed of intake channels and routing

## 16. Non-retaliation

No employee or customer is retaliated against for submitting or escalating an AI complaint.

## 17. Metrics

- Mean time to acknowledge
- Mean time to substantive response
- Resolution rate
- Category distribution
- Repeat complaints on same system
- Appeals filed and outcomes
- Regulator inquiries originating from complaints
