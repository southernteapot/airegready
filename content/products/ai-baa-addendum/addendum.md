# AI Addendum to HIPAA Business Associate Agreement

**Effective date:** [DATE]
**Covered Entity:** [NAME]
**Business Associate:** [VENDOR NAME]
**This Addendum supplements the Business Associate Agreement between the parties dated [DATE] (the "BAA")**

> **How to use this template.** An AI-specific addendum that supplements a HIPAA BAA. Use when your Business Associate provides AI features that process or produce Protected Health Information (PHI). Designed to close gaps that standard BAAs don't address — AI training, outputs as PHI, model retention. Have counsel review before use.

---

## 1. Definitions

All capitalized terms not defined here have the meanings in the BAA or in 45 CFR Parts 160 and 164.

- **"AI Features"** means the components of Business Associate's Services that use machine learning or generative AI.
- **"Training"** means any use of PHI to develop, fine-tune, or improve a model.
- **"AI Output"** means any material generated by the AI Features in response to inputs that contain or are derived from PHI.

## 2. Permitted and required uses and disclosures

Business Associate may Use and Disclose PHI only:

- To provide the Services, including AI Features, to Covered Entity
- As required by law
- For Business Associate's proper management and administration, consistent with 45 CFR § 164.504(e)

Business Associate will not Use or Disclose PHI for Training, for model improvement, or for product development, except with Covered Entity's prior written consent or where the data has been de-identified pursuant to 45 CFR § 164.514(a)-(b).

## 3. Safeguards

Business Associate will implement appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards consistent with the Security Rule. Safeguards for AI Features include:

- **Access controls** on training environments, prompts, and outputs
- **Encryption** of PHI in transit and at rest
- **Audit controls** that log AI-mediated Use and Disclosure of PHI
- **Integrity controls** for model versions and training data lineage
- **Workforce training** on PHI handling in AI contexts
- **AI-specific risks** addressed: prompt-injection exfiltration, cross-tenant leakage, memorization of PHI in trained weights

## 4. Subprocessors and subcontractors

Business Associate will not disclose PHI to any Subprocessor or subcontractor — including AI model providers, cloud hosts, retrieval services — except pursuant to a written agreement that:

- Imposes the same restrictions and obligations applicable to Business Associate under the BAA and this Addendum
- Prohibits the Subprocessor's Use of PHI for Training or other non-authorized purposes
- Provides for Covered Entity's rights of audit or equivalent assurance

Business Associate maintains a current list of Subprocessors touching PHI in **Annex A**.

## 5. AI Output treatment

AI Output that contains PHI, or is individually identifiable to a patient, is PHI. Business Associate will:

- Protect AI Output consistent with Security Rule safeguards
- Include AI Output in access, amendment, and accounting-of-disclosures workflows as PHI
- Delete AI Output upon termination consistent with Section 9

## 6. Patient rights (right of access, amendment, accounting)

Business Associate will, taking into account the nature of AI Features:

- Support Covered Entity's responses to Right of Access requests, including information about AI-mediated Uses of the requesting patient's PHI where technically feasible
- Support Amendment requests where technically feasible, and document limitations where not
- Support accounting-of-disclosures where PHI is shared through AI Features

## 7. Training data and model persistence

Where Covered Entity consents in writing to specific Training Uses of PHI:

- Business Associate will de-identify PHI prior to Training to the fullest extent consistent with the purpose
- Business Associate will document and maintain the consent, scope, and retention of Training data
- Business Associate acknowledges that PHI embedded in trained weights may create deletion / accounting limitations and will describe and mitigate them
- Business Associate will re-train or remove model artifacts if Covered Entity withdraws consent in writing, subject to reasonable implementation timelines

## 8. Breach notification

Business Associate will report to Covered Entity any Use or Disclosure of PHI not permitted by the BAA or this Addendum, any Security Incident, or any Breach of Unsecured PHI, without unreasonable delay and no later than [5 / 10] business days after discovery. Reports will include:

- Identification of affected individuals and PHI categories
- Nature of the incident and AI-specific vectors (e.g., prompt injection, cross-tenant leakage, model extraction)
- Remediation steps taken or proposed

Business Associate will cooperate with Covered Entity's Breach assessment, risk analysis, and notifications.

## 9. Termination and deletion

Upon termination of the BAA, Business Associate will:

- Return or destroy all PHI
- Delete PHI from active systems, backups (per retention schedule), AI caches, vector indices, and logs
- Certify deletion in writing
- Preserve PHI only where return or destruction is infeasible, in which case Business Associate will continue to apply protections

## 10. Audits and assurance

Covered Entity may audit Business Associate's compliance with this Addendum. Business Associate may offer current SOC 2 Type II, HITRUST, or equivalent assessment reports in lieu of on-site audit for non-incident reviews.

## 11. Regulatory compliance and AI-specific law

Business Associate will:

- Comply with HIPAA and HITECH Act requirements applicable to Business Associates
- Support Covered Entity's compliance with HHS Section 1557 Patient Care Decision Support Tool obligations by providing information on the AI Features' use of variables relating to race, color, national origin, sex, age, or disability, and mitigations
- Provide reasonable cooperation with Covered Entity's EU AI Act, Colorado AI Act, and state obligations where Covered Entity requests support

## 12. Conflict

In case of conflict between this Addendum and the BAA, this Addendum prevails for AI-specific matters. In case of conflict between either and HIPAA or applicable state law, the more protective requirement applies.
