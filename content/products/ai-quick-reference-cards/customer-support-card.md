# AI Quick Reference — Customer Support

**Keep this handy.** Full policy at [LINK]. Questions to [AI GOVERNANCE LEAD].

---

## The 5 rules

1. A human is accountable for the work — AI assists
2. Confidential stays confidential — don't paste into unapproved tools
3. Check the output — AI writes plausible wrong answers
4. Be honest about AI involvement when it matters
5. Follow the law — when in doubt, ask

---

## Green / Yellow / Red

- **Green** — Public FAQ, public docs, public troubleshooting guides → any approved tool
- **Yellow** — Internal macros, support playbooks → approved tools only
- **Red** — Customer names, accounts, tickets, payment/health/confidential content → **approved support tools only**

---

## What you can do

- Use approved AI assistants for drafting replies
- Summarize long ticket histories
- Translate customer messages
- Suggest troubleshooting paths from our knowledge base
- Categorize and route tickets
- Triage urgency (with human review)
- Generate QA rubrics

---

## What you can't do

- Paste customer personal information into public AI tools
- Send AI-generated replies without reading them first
- Promise outcomes AI made up ("Your refund will arrive in X days" when we haven't confirmed)
- Let AI invent policy ("our policy allows X" when it doesn't)
- Let an AI claim to be human if asked
- Bypass the handoff path when a customer asks for a person

---

## Customer-support-specific scenarios

**"AI suggests a reply — I'm on a busy shift — can I just send it?"** → Read it first. Check factual claims (dates, refund amounts, policy statements, product versions). Every sent message is yours.

**"A customer is upset. Can I use AI to draft an empathetic reply?"** → Yes, with review. But the customer deserves to feel heard. Personalize.

**"A customer asked our chatbot 'are you human?' "** → The chatbot must answer honestly. If you're handing off, tell them you're taking over.

**"Customer pasted what looks like their credit card into chat. What do I do?"** → Redact from the ticket, notify [PRIVACY / SECURITY LEAD], and ask the customer to never do that again. Never paste the raw ticket into an AI tool.

**"AI told me the customer's warranty is expired, but the customer insists it isn't."** → Check the system of record (CRM, billing). Don't rely on AI for policy or entitlement answers.

**"Can I use AI to write a QA review of my teammate's tickets?"** → For a rubric check, yes with review. For performance conclusions about your teammate, no — that's an HR process.

## Chatbot rules

- **Disclose** — "I'm an AI assistant from [COMPANY NAME]" up front
- **Handoff** — "agent" or "human" must route to a person; handoff is confirmed to the customer
- **Honesty** — bot must not claim to be a human if asked
- **Scope** — bot limits are communicated; users know what the bot can't do
- **Logging** — transcripts are logged for quality; customers are informed

## Escalation

Escalate to [SUPPORT LEAD] or [LEGAL / PRIVACY LEAD] if:

- A customer asks formal questions about bias, explanations of decisions, or data use
