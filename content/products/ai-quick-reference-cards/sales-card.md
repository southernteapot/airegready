## The 5 rules

1. A human is accountable for the work — AI assists
2. Confidential stays confidential — don't paste into unapproved tools
3. Check the output — AI writes plausible wrong answers
4. Be honest about AI involvement when it matters
5. Follow the law — when in doubt, ask

---

## Green / Yellow / Red

- **Green** — Public customer logos, published reviews, public pricing → any approved tool
- **Yellow** — Internal playbooks, win-loss notes, anonymized pipeline data → approved tools only
- **Red** — Customer lists, contract terms, NDA-covered info, individual contact details → **approved CRM/tools only**

---

## What you can do

- Draft prospect outreach emails (with human review)
- Summarize customer calls (with participant consent)
- Research prospects using public sources
- Build objection-handling scripts
- Draft proposal sections from approved templates
- Analyze your own pipeline
- Translate customer communications

---

## What you can't do

- Paste customer names, emails, contract terms, or account details into public AI tools
- Use AI to impersonate a customer in a test or demo
- Fabricate testimonials, case studies, references, or metrics
- Make commitments to customers on behalf of AI ("our AI will handle that") that we can't deliver
- Use AI to draft claims about our products that haven't been reviewed by marketing/legal
- Cold-email people with AI-generated content that claims to be personal when it isn't
- Use AI to screen customer creditworthiness, insurance eligibility, or other consequential decisions without oversight

## Sales-specific scenarios

**"Can I use AI to personalize cold emails?"** → Yes, in approved tools. Use our subscriber / prospect list rules. If the message is automated enough that a reasonable recipient would expect a personal human, include an automated-message disclosure.

**"Can I use AI to build a 'company brief' on a prospect?"** → Yes from public sources. Don't paste private information about the prospect into a public AI tool — including emails they sent us.

**"Can I use AI to draft my call summary in the CRM?"** → Yes, in approved tools. Review for accuracy. Recorded calls require participant consent.

**"AI voice agents for outbound calls — OK?"** → Multiple state laws require disclosure (AI voice robocalls covered by CA AB 2905, Texas TRAIGA, others). Check with Legal before deploying.

**"Can I use AI to decide which leads to prioritize?"** → Scoring is fine if the tool is approved, the data is handled per policy, and a human makes contact decisions. Avoid any scoring that uses protected-class proxies.

**"Can I create a fake customer testimonial with AI?"** → No. FTC, state consumer protection laws, and our own ethics prohibit this.

**"A prospect asked if they're talking to a bot."** → Answer honestly. If it's a bot, say so. Offer a human path.

---

## Legal guardrails

- **Bot disclosure** — CA, TX, and EU require clear disclosure of bot identity in sales contexts reaching those jurisdictions
- **FCRA** — credit-based sales (cards, lending, insurance) have adverse-action rules
- **TCPA / state do-not-call** — AI-assisted outreach must respect opt-outs and DNC lists
- **GDPR / CCPA** — prospect data is personal data; handle per privacy policy

---

## Good practice

- Keep an audit trail of which AI tool was used for which communication
- Review AI-generated research for accuracy before sending to a prospect
- Disclose AI assistance in sensitive contexts (legal, medical, high-stakes procurement)
- Don't let AI make a promise we can't keep — review generated commitment language carefully
