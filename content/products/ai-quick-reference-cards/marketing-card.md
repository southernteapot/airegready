# AI Quick Reference — Marketing

**Keep this handy.** The full policy is at [LINK]. When in doubt, ask [AI GOVERNANCE LEAD].

---

## The 5 rules

1. A human is accountable for the work — AI assists
2. Confidential stays confidential — don't paste into unapproved tools
3. Check the output — AI writes plausible wrong answers
4. Be honest about AI involvement when it matters
5. Follow the law — when in doubt, ask

---

## Green / Yellow / Red

- **Green** — Public market research, public competitor pages, published stats → any approved tool
- **Yellow** — Internal campaign strategy, non-sensitive analytics → approved tools only
- **Red** — Customer lists, subscriber data, unreleased product info, private analytics → **stop. Ask first.**

---

## What you can do

- Draft headlines, body copy, emails, and social posts (with human review)
- Generate first-draft images for decks, internal mockups, social
- Transcribe and summarize meetings (with consent)
- Localize copy between languages
- Brainstorm angles, hooks, campaign concepts
- Analyze public competitor messaging
- Generate variants for A/B testing

---

## What you can't do

- Invent customer testimonials or quotes
- Use real person's likeness, voice, or name without consent
- Make unsubstantiated claims about our products ("AI-powered" when it isn't, "patented" when it isn't)
- Paste customer/subscriber lists into public AI tools
- Generate content that infringes copyright or trademarks (styles of specific living artists, branded imagery)
- Auto-publish AI content without human review
- Use AI to generate political or election-related content without legal review (CA, TX, and others regulate this)

---

## Marketing-specific scenarios

**"Can I use AI to write a customer case study?"** → You can draft one with AI help. Facts, quotes, metrics must come from the customer, with their approval. Never let AI invent quotes.

**"What about AI-generated images for our blog?"** → Generally fine if the image doesn't depict real people without consent, doesn't closely mimic a living artist's distinctive style, and includes our standard licensing review. Disclose AI generation on editorial content where authorship matters.

**"Can I use an AI headshot of our CEO for social?"** → Only with explicit consent from the person depicted, and only for approved purposes. AI portraits of real people are high-risk.

**"Can I use AI to personalize emails at scale?"** → Yes, in approved tools only, with our subscriber data policy. Disclose that the message is automated where the recipient wouldn't reasonably expect personal human composition.

**"I wrote a landing page with AI. Is it OK to publish?"** → Review for factual accuracy, brand voice, and any claims about capabilities. Run through our normal editorial and legal review for any new product claims.

---

## Legal claim warnings

- **"Industry-leading"** → needs substantiation
- **"Proven to X"** → needs cited data
- **"Patented"** → needs actual patent
- **"AI-powered"** → needs to be true (FTC focus area)
- **"No other [X] can"** → almost always overreach
- **Testimonials, reviews, case studies** → must be authentic, verifiable, with disclosures

---

## Disclosure

- **AI-generated imagery** in editorial contexts → disclose
- **AI-drafted content** in contexts where authorship matters → disclose
- **AI customer communications** → chatbot/voice disclosure up front
- **Election / political content** → legal review before publishing
