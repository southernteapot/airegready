## Preamble

At [COMPANY NAME], we use artificial intelligence because we believe it can do real good — save our customers time, help them make better decisions, and make our products and services stronger. We also know AI can cause harm when built or used carelessly. This Code of Ethics sets the principles we follow to capture the good and prevent the harm.

We commit these principles to writing so that:

- Our team understands what's expected
- Our customers know what to expect from us
- Our partners can hold us to these standards
- Our future selves can be held accountable to our past selves

---

## Our principles

### 1. People first

AI serves people. People don't serve AI. When our interests and the interests of the individuals affected by our AI diverge, we err toward the people affected.

### 2. Accountability belongs to humans

A human is responsible for every AI system we build or use. AI is a tool — powerful, but a tool. "The AI decided" is never our answer.

### 3. Honesty

We say truthful things about what our AI does and doesn't do. We don't exaggerate capabilities. We don't hide AI involvement where it matters. We correct promptly when we get something wrong.

### 4. Fairness

We test for bias before we deploy and while we operate. When we find disparities, we mitigate, retire, or disclose — we don't hide. AI is not a way to launder discrimination.

### 5. Privacy

We minimize personal data in AI systems. We honor the rights people have in their data. We treat AI systems as subject to privacy law, not outside it.

### 6. Safety

We test our AI for ways it could cause harm. We build safeguards against misuse. We respond when safeguards fail.

### 7. Transparency

We explain how our AI works in plain language. We disclose AI involvement where reasonable users would want to know. We support customers in meeting their own transparency obligations.

### 8. Oversight

We don't trust AI with decisions that affect people's livelihoods, rights, health, safety, or dignity without human review. We keep humans in the loop where it matters.

### 9. Security

We protect our AI systems from attack, theft, and misuse. We treat AI infrastructure with the same seriousness as any critical system.

### 10. Continuous learning

The technology is new and moves fast. The law is catching up. We commit to keep learning, update our practices, and correct course when we're wrong.

---

## What we will not do

- Build AI specifically to deceive, manipulate, or exploit
- Deploy AI in ways that are prohibited by applicable law
- Build or deploy AI for:
  - Social scoring
  - Mass surveillance of innocent people
  - Generating CSAM or non-consensual intimate imagery
  - Weapons of mass harm
  - Emotion inference in workplaces or schools (except for safety / medical reasons)
- Misrepresent our AI capabilities to customers, investors, or regulators
- Use customer data to train AI without the customer's informed consent
- Hide AI use from people who would reasonably want to know

## How we will operate

- **Governance**: named accountability, regular review, documented processes
- **Framework**: our AI Governance Framework turns these principles into operating practices
- **Policies**: our AI Acceptable Use Policy, Data Governance Policy, Incident Response Plan, and related documents make the details workable for everyone on our team
- **Training**: every employee with access to AI tools is trained
- **Measurement**: we measure our AI posture and report on it internally and to our board
- **Improvement**: we treat AI as a program, not a project — continuous improvement is expected

## Our commitments to specific groups

### To our customers

- We will not train AI on your data unless you opt in
- We will disclose how AI is used in features that affect you
- We will respond to your concerns and requests
- We will support your own compliance obligations with reasonable information

### To our employees

- We will train you on AI responsibly
- We will not use AI to make termination or discipline decisions without qualified human review
- We will not monitor your work using AI without notice and proportionality
- We will hear your concerns about AI and act on them

### To the public

- We will not use AI in ways that disparately harm vulnerable groups
- We will maintain a channel for reporting concerns
- We will engage with regulators constructively

### To regulators

- We will cooperate with inquiries in good faith
- We will maintain records demonstrating how we apply these principles
- We will report serious incidents as required

## Who holds us accountable

- **The Board** or governing body reviews AI posture regularly
- **The AI Governance Lead** implements these principles day-to-day
- **The AI Review Committee** reviews deployments against these principles
- **The CEO** commits the company to these principles
- **Every employee** is expected to raise concerns when practice diverges from principles
- **The public** can submit feedback at [CONTACT]

## Review

This Code will be reviewed at least annually. Material changes will be communicated to employees, customers, and — as applicable — regulators.

## Closing
