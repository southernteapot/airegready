# AI in Legal Practice — Readiness Checklist

**Completed by:** ______________________________

## Phase 1 — Scope and inventory

- [ ] **1.1** Inventory of every AI tool used in the firm or department (research, drafting, eDiscovery, contract analysis, practice management, marketing, billing)
- [ ] **1.2** Each tool classified by use case and risk
- [ ] **1.3** Matter-specific AI use tagged in matter records
- [ ] **1.4** Cloud / on-prem / client-portal status per tool

## Phase 2 — Firm AI policy

- [ ] **2.1** Written AI policy covering competence, confidentiality, supervision, fees, disclosure, and court rules
- [ ] **2.2** Approved tools list published
- [ ] **2.3** Unauthorized tools flagged for escalation
- [ ] **2.4** Annual review and update

## Phase 3 — Competence (Rule 1.1)

- [ ] **3.1** AI training for every attorney
- [ ] **3.2** AI training for paralegals and legal assistants
- [ ] **3.3** CLE credits tracked where available
- [ ] **3.4** Competence maintenance documented (ongoing literacy, not one-and-done)

## Phase 4 — Confidentiality (Rule 1.6)

- [ ] **4.1** Approved AI tools vetted for no-training-on-inputs
- [ ] **4.2** DPA or enterprise agreement in place with every AI vendor
- [ ] **4.3** Prohibition on pasting client-confidential info into consumer AI tools
- [ ] **4.4** Client consent process for AI uses that require it
- [ ] **4.5** Conflict checking applies to shared AI tool usage (multi-tenant risks)

## Phase 5 — Communication (Rule 1.4)

- [ ] **5.1** Engagement letters address AI use
- [ ] **5.2** Client consent captured where material
- [ ] **5.3** Client disclosure when AI used in material work

## Phase 6 — Supervision (Rules 5.1, 5.3)

- [ ] **6.1** Partners/supervising attorneys understand AI tools used on their matters
- [ ] **6.2** Written supervisory standards for AI-assisted work
- [ ] **6.3** Quality review for AI-drafted documents
- [ ] **6.4** Non-lawyer staff training and oversight

## Phase 7 — Candor to tribunal (Rule 3.3)

- [ ] **7.1** Citation verification protocol mandatory for AI-assisted filings
- [ ] **7.2** Fact-check and quote verification
- [ ] **7.3** Signed attestation by drafting attorney on verification
- [ ] **7.4** Process to correct erroneous filings promptly

## Phase 8 — Court rules

- [ ] **8.1** Standing orders tracked by court for every jurisdiction the firm practices in
- [ ] **8.2** Disclosure language drafted for courts requiring AI disclosure
- [ ] **8.3** Monitoring process for new standing orders

## Phase 9 — Fees (Rule 1.5)

- [ ] **9.1** Fee arrangements reviewed for AI-era reasonableness
- [ ] **9.2** Client disclosure of AI-assisted work and billing
- [ ] **9.3** Rebilling of AI tool costs addressed explicitly
- [ ] **9.4** Value-based or efficiency-sharing arrangements considered

## Phase 10 — Unauthorized practice of law (Rule 5.5)

- [ ] **10.1** Consumer-facing AI products reviewed for UPL
- [ ] **10.2** Chatbots scoped to information, not advice
- [ ] **10.3** Multi-state practice implications analyzed

## Phase 11 — Privilege and work product

- [ ] **11.1** Vendor contracts support privilege preservation
- [ ] **11.2** Training on privilege implications of AI use
- [ ] **11.3** Separate secure environments for privileged vs. non-privileged work

## Phase 12 — Data security

- [ ] **12.1** Reasonable security measures under Formal Opinion 477R
- [ ] **12.2** Incident response plan addresses AI incidents
- [ ] **12.3** State breach notification mapped
- [ ] **12.4** Cyber insurance coverage reviewed for AI exposure

## Phase 13 — Vendor management

- [ ] **13.1** Vendor risk assessment for every AI tool
- [ ] **13.2** Vendor documentation retained
- [ ] **13.3** Renewal and change review

## Phase 14 — Incident handling

- [ ] **14.1** Hallucinated case/citation found in draft — quick-response procedure
- [ ] **14.2** Hallucinated content in filing — court/opposing-counsel notice protocol
- [ ] **14.3** Client data leak via AI — client and regulator notice procedure
- [ ] **14.4** Post-incident review

## Phase 15 — EU AI Act / CAIA (if in scope)

- [ ] **15.1** Annex III high-risk assessment (justice administration / legal services)
- [ ] **15.2** Colorado AI Act consumer-facing legal-services obligations

## Phase 16 — Records

- [ ] **16.1** Training records
- [ ] **16.2** Vendor due diligence
- [ ] **16.3** Matter-specific AI use log
- [ ] **16.4** Citation verification records for AI-assisted filings
