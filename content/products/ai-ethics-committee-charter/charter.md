# AI Ethics Committee — Charter

**Company:** [COMPANY NAME]
**Effective date:** [DATE]
**Owner:** [Chair — typically the AI Governance Lead or designated executive]
**Review:** annual

> **How to use this charter.** Establishes an AI Ethics Committee with authority, membership, cadence, and scope. Optional addition to the AI Governance Framework for organizations that want a dedicated ethics review (often helpful for consumer-facing AI, AI affecting vulnerable groups, and high-stakes sectors). For smaller organizations, ethics review can be folded into the AI Review Committee; for larger organizations, a separate Ethics Committee provides distinct perspective.

---

## 1. Purpose

The AI Ethics Committee provides independent judgment on the ethical implications of AI development and deployment at [COMPANY NAME]. Where the AI Review Committee evaluates feasibility, legal compliance, and risk mitigation, the Ethics Committee evaluates alignment with our AI Code of Ethics and broader social values.

## 2. Authority

The Committee:

- **Reviews** proposed AI uses referred to it (see Section 6)
- **Recommends** approval, conditions, or rejection on ethical grounds
- **Advises** leadership on ethical questions the AI program raises
- **Escalates** concerns to the Executive Sponsor and Board when warranted

The Committee's recommendations are **binding** for the categories listed in Section 5.2; **advisory** elsewhere.

## 3. Independence

To preserve independence:

- The Chair does not report to the head of product or revenue
- At least [2] committee members are external / independent (subject matter experts, ethicists, community representatives)
- Members rotate on a staggered schedule
- Members disclose conflicts of interest
- Members are indemnified for good-faith service

## 4. Membership

### Composition

- **Chair** — [AI Governance Lead / designated executive]
- **Legal / Ethics Officer**
- **Privacy Officer**
- **Research / Engineering representative**
- **Product representative**
- **HR representative** (for employment-adjacent matters)
- **Customer advocate / Support lead**
- **[2+] External members** (academic, civil-society, community, domain expert)
- **Executive Sponsor** (as observer)

### Terms

- 2-year terms, renewable once
- Staggered rotations
- Chair appointed by [CEO / Board]
- External members appointed with [Board / ethics advisor] input

### Quorum

- [5 of 9] with at least one external member present
- External members may be remote

## 5. Scope

### 5.1 Matters that must be referred to the Ethics Committee

- Novel AI use cases not previously deployed by [COMPANY NAME]
- AI systems affecting vulnerable populations (children, patients, applicants in protected classes, incarcerated people, immigrants, others)
- AI systems involving physical safety or life-altering decisions
- AI systems using biometric data
- AI systems using synthetic-media generation capable of depicting real persons
- AI systems used in law enforcement, military, or intelligence contexts
- Research proposals using AI involving human subjects
- AI partnerships with controversial third parties
- Any system the AI Review Committee flags for ethical review

### 5.2 Binding vs. advisory

- **Binding**: prohibited uses under our Code of Ethics; deployment of AI in vulnerable-population contexts; AI-in-employment-decisions escalations; biometric use cases; deepfake capabilities
- **Advisory**: other matters

### 5.3 Not in scope

- Routine product decisions not implicating ethics
- Operational matters (reviewed elsewhere)
- Individual personnel matters (HR)

## 6. Operation

### 6.1 Intake

- Proposal submitted to Chair with: use case, population affected, ethics issues identified, pre-mitigations proposed
- Chair schedules review; urgent matters can be convened ad hoc

### 6.2 Review meetings

- Cadence: monthly (scheduled); ad hoc as needed
- Minutes published to members and leadership
- Sensitive matters may be summarized

### 6.3 Deliberation

- Review the AI Code of Ethics principles against the proposal
- Examine impacts on affected populations
- Consider alternatives
- Vote; dissent recorded
- Recommendations documented

### 6.4 Recommendations

Categories:

- **Approve** — proceed as proposed
- **Approve with conditions** — must meet specified conditions before or during deployment
- **Defer** — more information needed
- **Reject** — do not proceed; record reasons

### 6.5 Dissent and escalation

- Any member may register formal dissent; recorded in minutes
- Dissents with substantial ethical weight escalate to Executive Sponsor
- Any Committee member can escalate directly to the Board via the Chair when they believe a matter warrants it

## 7. Reporting

### 7.1 Internal

- Monthly summary to AI Review Committee and leadership
- Quarterly report to Executive Sponsor
- Annual report to the Board or governing body

### 7.2 External (as appropriate)

- Summary of ethics reviews in Trust Center publications
- Disclosure in regulatory filings where relevant

### 7.3 Transparency

- The existence and general scope of the Committee is public
- Specific deliberations are confidential unless parties agree to disclosure

## 8. Conflicts of interest

- Members disclose conflicts at appointment and annually
- Members recuse for specific matters where conflicts arise
- Financial conflicts require recusal; other conflicts may require disclosure only
- Chair manages conflict process; external member can raise concerns

## 9. Meetings and procedure

- Meetings held in person or remotely
- Agenda circulated [5] business days in advance
- Supporting materials included
- Minutes by scribe
- Materials retained per records policy

## 10. Resources

- Access to relevant documents, data, and personnel
- Authority to commission external expert reviews
- Budget for training, travel, external members' honoraria, specialized advisors

## 11. Training

- Members receive onboarding on AI fundamentals, applicable law, ethics frameworks, and [COMPANY NAME] context
- Continuing education as the field evolves

## 12. Protection for members and reporters

- Good-faith members indemnified by [COMPANY NAME]
- Whistleblower protections for employees raising concerns
- Non-retaliation commitment

## 13. Amendments

This Charter may be amended by [CEO / Board] with consultation of current Committee.

## 14. Reporting to the Board / Audit Committee

Matters rising to the Board level include:

- Material concerns about systemic ethical practices
- Irreconcilable disagreements with Executive Sponsor
- Major recommended policy changes
- Annual report

## 15. Closure

The Committee exists to help [COMPANY NAME] apply its stated principles. It is not a compliance theater. If the Committee is marginalized, ignored, or reduced to rubber-stamp, members should say so openly — and leadership should listen.
