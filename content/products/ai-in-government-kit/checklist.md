## Phase 1 — Governance

- [ ] **1.1** Chief AI Officer (or equivalent) designated
- [ ] **1.2** AI governance body chartered
- [ ] **1.3** NIST AI RMF adopted as framework
- [ ] **1.4** Policy and procedures documented

## Phase 2 — Inventory

- [ ] **2.1** Comprehensive AI Use Case Inventory
- [ ] **2.2** Public-facing version where required
- [ ] **2.3** Classification: rights-impacting / safety-impacting / administrative
- [ ] **2.4** Updated at least annually

## Phase 3 — Impact assessment (rights-impacting)

- [ ] **3.1** Pre-deployment assessment completed
- [ ] **3.2** Consultation with affected communities where applicable
- [ ] **3.3** Disparate-impact analysis across protected classes
- [ ] **3.4** Alternatives analysis
- [ ] **3.5** Mitigation plan
- [ ] **3.6** Reassessment cadence

## Phase 4 — Human alternative and opt-out

- [ ] **4.1** Human alternative for rights-impacting decisions
- [ ] **4.2** Opt-out process where applicable
- [ ] **4.3** Appeal pathway

## Phase 5 — Public notice

- [ ] **5.1** Notice to individuals subject to AI-assisted decisions
- [ ] **5.2** Plain-language explanations
- [ ] **5.3** Public inventory entries
- [ ] **5.4** Feedback mechanism

## Phase 6 — Procurement

- [ ] **6.1** AI acquisition standards applied
- [ ] **6.2** Vendor transparency required (model documentation, bias testing)
- [ ] **6.3** Data rights and IP clarified
- [ ] **6.4** Section 508 accessibility (federal)
- [ ] **6.5** Cooperation with FRIA / impact assessments
- [ ] **6.6** Incident notification obligations

## Phase 7 — Cybersecurity

- [ ] **7.1** FedRAMP / equivalent authorization for vendor systems (federal)
- [ ] **7.2** FISMA / equivalent ATO
- [ ] **7.3** NIST CSF alignment
- [ ] **7.4** Incident response tied to CIRCIA and sector rules

## Phase 8 — Records

- [ ] **8.1** AI artifacts retained per Federal Records Act / state equivalents
- [ ] **8.2** FOIA preparedness: models, prompts, outputs, decisions accessible
- [ ] **8.3** Vendor confidentiality claims pre-analyzed

## Phase 9 — Constitutional / anti-discrimination

- [ ] **9.1** Due process review for rights-affecting AI
- [ ] **9.2** Equal protection / disparate impact testing
- [ ] **9.3** First Amendment considerations for moderation / surveillance
- [ ] **9.4** Fourth Amendment / warrant review for surveillance AI
- [ ] **9.5** Title VI / Title IX / Section 504 applied

## Phase 10 — Sector-specific

- [ ] **10.1** Social services: benefits, child welfare, unemployment rules
- [ ] **10.2** Education: FERPA / PPRA / IDEA
- [ ] **10.3** Healthcare: HIPAA / Medicaid / Medicare
- [ ] **10.4** Criminal justice: specific statutory and constitutional limits, court requirements

## Phase 11 — EU AI Act (if EU jurisdiction)

- [ ] **11.1** Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment (Art 27) for public body deployers
- [ ] **11.2** EU database registration (Art 49(1a))
- [ ] **11.3** Transparency obligations

## Phase 12 — Training and oversight

- [ ] **12.1** Staff training on AI governance
- [ ] **12.2** Ethics / AI literacy requirements
- [ ] **12.3** Oversight body reporting cadence

## Phase 13 — Incident response

- [ ] **13.1** Plan tuned for government AI scenarios (wrongful denial, biased outcome, system outage)
- [ ] **13.2** Oversight body and media response
- [ ] **13.3** Affected-person notification
