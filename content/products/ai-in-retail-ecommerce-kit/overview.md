# AI in Retail & E-commerce — Compliance Kit Overview

**Regulatory landscape:**

- **FTC Act Section 5** — unfair or deceptive practices; AI-washing; dark patterns
- **Consumer Protection laws (state)** — California, New York, Washington, and others
- **Pricing and advertising laws** — price-fixing antitrust rules if coordination
- **CCPA/CPRA, GDPR** — personalization and profiling
- **CAN-SPAM, TCPA** — marketing channels
- **Payment Card Industry (PCI)** — AI touching payment data
- **Children's Online Privacy (COPPA, CA AADC)** — retail reaching children
- **State recycling / right-to-repair** — sometimes intersect with AI product telemetry
- **EU AI Act** — transparency for chatbots; some pricing AI scrutiny
- **CA SB 942** — synthetic content transparency in brand media
- **State biometric laws** (BIPA in Illinois) — in-store cameras, voice, fingerprint

> **How to use this kit.** For e-commerce brands, online marketplaces, brick-and-mortar retailers with digital operations, and retail tech vendors. Lighter-touch than hiring or healthcare AI, but regulatory exposure is real and growing.

## 1. AI use cases in retail

- Search and recommendation
- Dynamic pricing and personalization
- Chatbots and voice assistants
- Fraud detection and account takeover prevention
- Inventory, supply chain, and demand forecasting
- Marketing (covered separately in the Marketing Kit)
- Fulfillment and logistics optimization
- In-store analytics (cameras, foot-traffic, heat maps)
- Loyalty and retention AI
- AI-generated product descriptions, reviews, and images
- AI shopping assistants and virtual try-on

## 2. FTC and consumer protection

- **Dark patterns** — AI-driven UX that steers users into unwanted choices is under FTC scrutiny
- **Fake reviews** — FTC's 2024 final rule prohibits fake and AI-generated reviews
- **Subscription traps** — AI that optimizes retention must comply with ROSCA and state subscription laws
- **AI-washing** — product claims involving AI must be substantiated
- **Price discrimination** — AI personalization can raise consumer-protection and anti-discrimination issues

## 3. Fake-review rule (FTC, 2024)

Prohibits:

- Fabricated reviews
- Reviews by AI without clear disclosure
- Buying / selling reviews
- Review suppression
- Insider reviews without disclosure of material connection

Retailers operating review systems have duties to prevent and remove offending content.

## 4. Dynamic pricing considerations

- Price personalization based on protected classes is unlawful
- Price personalization based on other signals may be legal but risks consumer backlash
- Surge-pricing algorithms in essential goods can draw AG attention
- Coordinated pricing among retailers using shared AI risks antitrust

## 5. Recommendation and search AI

- Algorithmic transparency norms emerging (EU Digital Services Act for larger platforms)
- Accessibility — recommendations must be usable by all, including assistive tech users
- Child-facing recommendations have special rules (COPPA, CA AADC)

## 6. Chatbots and voice assistants

- EU AI Act Art 50 — disclose AI status
- CA SB 1001 — bot disclosure in commercial contexts
- TX TRAIGA — disclosure in certain contexts
- Claims made by chatbots bind the retailer — watch commitments on pricing, availability, returns

## 7. Fraud / account takeover AI

- Balance fraud prevention with customer experience
- False positives affect real customers — remedy path required
- Retention of fraud models must support disputes and appeals
- Avoid over-reliance on biometric factors that implicate BIPA or other state biometric laws

## 8. In-store analytics and biometrics

- Cameras with face detection: state laws vary; Illinois BIPA is a notable exposure
- Consent requirements for biometric identifiers
- Posting notices and signage
- Retention and destruction schedules

## 9. AI-generated content on site

- Product descriptions generated by AI should be accurate; check for hallucinations in specs
- AI-generated images in editorial contexts — disclose where authorship matters
- Review content generated by AI — disclose per FTC fake review rule

## 10. Customer service AI

- Returns, cancellations, warranty, refund rights — chatbots giving wrong answers create liability
- Accessibility of chatbot interfaces
- Escalation paths to humans

## 11. Children's commerce

- COPPA for under-13 users
- CA AADC / state age-appropriate design codes for under-18 users
- Age-appropriate design
- Age verification with privacy safeguards

## 12. Practical readiness path

1. Inventory retail AI
2. Review reviews / ratings for FTC fake-review-rule compliance
3. Dynamic pricing audit
4. Chatbot disclosure and accuracy testing
5. Fraud-model remedy path
6. In-store biometric consent and signage (where applicable)
7. AI content accuracy checks
8. Recommendation fairness and accessibility
9. Subscription retention AI audited for dark patterns and state subscription laws
10. Training for customer service, marketing, and operations teams

## 13. Common traps

- **AI-written fake reviews** — illegal, operationally tempting, heavily enforced
- **Dark patterns** in AI-driven UX
- **BIPA exposure** from in-store biometric analytics
- **Chatbot price mistakes** becoming company-binding
- **Subscription cancellation traps** optimized by AI
- **Unsupported AI claims** in product listings or marketing
