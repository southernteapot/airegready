# ISO/IEC 42001 — Readiness Kit Overview

**Standard:** ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS)
**Issuing body:** International Organization for Standardization / International Electrotechnical Commission
**Certifiability:** Yes — AIMS certifications are available through accredited certification bodies

> **How to use this kit.** For organizations pursuing formal AI management system certification, or using ISO 42001 as a scaffold to build a rigorous AI program. Pair with the NIST AI RMF Implementation Pack (the two frameworks are complementary) and the AI Governance Framework.

---

## 1. Why ISO 42001

ISO/IEC 42001 is the first international management-system standard for AI. It follows the "high-level structure" of ISO 9001 / 27001, so organizations already holding those certifications can add 42001 with less friction.

Reasons to pursue:

- **Certification** is increasingly expected by enterprise customers and public-sector buyers
- **Structured approach** gives you a complete management system you can audit internally and externally
- **EU AI Act alignment** — 42001 aligns with the QMS requirements in Art 17 of the EU AI Act for high-risk AI providers
- **Colorado AI Act** — named as one path to the rebuttable presumption of compliance
- **Continuous improvement** ethos fits AI's rapid change

## 2. Structure of ISO 42001

Following the ISO management system pattern:

- **Clause 4** — Context of the organization
- **Clause 5** — Leadership
- **Clause 6** — Planning
- **Clause 7** — Support
- **Clause 8** — Operation
- **Clause 9** — Performance evaluation
- **Clause 10** — Improvement
- **Annex A** — 38 controls (comparable to ISO 27001 Annex A) organized into AI-specific control areas

## 3. Annex A controls — the substantive meat

Control areas in Annex A:

- **Policies related to AI** (A.2)
- **Internal organization** (A.3)
- **Resources for AI systems** (A.4)
- **Assessing impacts of AI systems** (A.5)
- **AI system life cycle** (A.6) — development, data, deployment
- **Data for AI systems** (A.7)
- **Information for interested parties of AI systems** (A.8)
- **Use of AI systems** (A.9)
- **Third-party and customer relationships** (A.10)

Each control has a statement of objective and suggested implementation guidance.

## 4. Certification path

1. **Gap analysis** — assess current state against the standard
2. **Implementation** — build / update the management system to cover gaps
3. **Internal audit** — verify the AIMS is functioning
4. **Management review** — leadership reviews AIMS effectiveness
5. **Stage 1 certification audit** — readiness review by certification body
6. **Stage 2 certification audit** — full audit of implementation
7. **Surveillance audits** — typically annual
8. **Recertification** — typically 3 years

Timeline: 6-12 months for most mid-size organizations from gap analysis to certification.

## 5. Relationship to other frameworks

| Framework | Relationship to ISO 42001 |
| --- | --- |
| **ISO 27001** | Information security — complementary; many controls overlap |
| **ISO 9001** | Quality management — same management system structure |
| **NIST AI RMF** | Functional companion — RMF provides the "what to measure" that ISO 42001 embeds into a management system |
| **EU AI Act** | Significantly overlaps Art 17 QMS; certification is often cited as evidence of compliance |
| **SOC 2** | Different model (point-in-time assurance vs. management system); AI-specific controls can be added as an SOC 2 criteria extension |

## 7. Practical readiness path

1. **Scope the AIMS** — systems, processes, people in scope
2. **Context of the organization** (Clause 4) — interested parties, issues, boundaries
3. **Leadership** (Clause 5) — policy, roles, commitment
4. **Planning** (Clause 6) — AI risk assessment and treatment plan
5. **Support** (Clause 7) — resources, competence, awareness, communication, documented information
6. **Operation** (Clause 8) — how AI is developed, procured, deployed, monitored
7. **Performance evaluation** (Clause 9) — monitoring, internal audit, management review
8. **Improvement** (Clause 10) — nonconformity and corrective action, continual improvement
9. **Annex A controls** — implementation and statement of applicability

## 8. Common traps

- Treating 42001 as a checkbox exercise without real change to how AI is governed
- Focusing on Annex A controls without the management system clauses
- Skipping the AI impact assessment (Annex A.5) — a central expectation
- Not integrating with existing ISO 27001 where you have it
- Hiring auditors who don't actually know AI
