# Regulatory Update Subscription — Product Framework

**Product type:** Recurring subscription
**Target price:** [$19-49] / month
**Audience:** Existing customers who want continuous updates + a broader AI-regulatory-news feed
**Competitor reference:** internal legal watch newsletters at large firms; GRC platform subscriptions

> **This document is the internal product framework** — content structure, operations, and roadmap. Customer-facing copy lives elsewhere.

---

## Why a subscription

One-time product purchases work for most buyers. But AI regulation is moving fast enough that a meaningful share of customers — active AI programs, compliance teams, enterprise accounts — need ongoing updates. A subscription also:

- Creates recurring revenue
- Strengthens the relationship with buyers beyond a single transaction
- Justifies ongoing content investment
- Natural fit with the Reseller License market (consultants bundle updates into client engagements)

## What the subscription delivers

### 1. Product updates (continuous)

- Any AIRegReady product owned by the subscriber is updated for as long as the subscription is active
- Notifications when updates are published
- Change-log access per product (what changed, why, when)

### 2. Monthly Regulatory Briefing

- **Published:** first business day of each month
- **Delivery:** email + web archive
- **Length:** 2,000-4,000 words + structured sections
- **Contents:**
  - **Executive summary** — top 3-5 things that changed this month
  - **U.S. federal** — enforcement actions, agency guidance, legislative developments
  - **U.S. state-by-state** — new laws, amendments, AG actions, implementing rules
  - **EU** — AI Office activity, EU AI Act implementing acts, harmonized standards
  - **UK / Commonwealth** — ICO, FCA, Ofcom, other sector regulators
  - **Sector highlights** — rotating focus on healthcare, financial services, education, etc.
  - **Case to watch** — one ongoing enforcement action or lawsuit worth tracking
  - **One month ahead** — what's expected next month
  - **Links** — primary sources, analysis, our relevant products

### 3. Change-log for products

Per-product log showing:

- Each update date and version
- What changed (high level)
- Why it changed (regulation, audit finding, customer feedback)
- Relevant primary sources

Lets subscribers see exactly what's different since they last downloaded.

### 4. Early access

- Draft versions of upcoming new products
- Preview and feedback window (1-2 weeks) before general release
- Subscribers get the final version free (within their subscription term)

### 5. Community Q&A (optional — pending subscriber volume)

- Monthly online office hours
- Submit questions in advance; addressed live
- Archive of answered questions

### 6. Priority support

- Subscribers' emails answered within 1 business day
- First line of customer support

## Content operations

### Editorial cadence

- **Weekly scan** — regulatory news gathering (internal)
- **Monthly brief** — written in week 1; reviewed in week 2; published first business day
- **Update cycle for products** — continuous; major updates announced monthly alongside brief
- **Quarterly deep-dive** — longer analytical piece on one topic

### Sources

- Official regulator sites (FTC, CFPB, SEC, state AGs, DPAs, EU AI Office, sector regulators)
- Court filings and judgments
- Reputable news (with attribution)
- Law firm alerts (with attribution)
- Academic / policy research
- Customer feedback on emerging questions

### Editorial standards

- Plain English
- Cite primary sources
- No legal advice
- Neutral tone
- Acknowledge uncertainty where it exists
- Disclose our own product commercial connection where relevant

### Review

- Every brief reviewed by [EDITOR + SME]
- Fact-check on critical assertions
- Plain-language pass before publishing

- Announce to existing customers only
- Price introductory tier
- Collect 50-100 subscribers
- Refine content cadence and format based on feedback

### Phase 2 — General availability (M+4)

- Public landing page
- Upsell in checkout

- Full price list

### Phase 3 — Enterprise tier (M+6 or when demand warrants)

- Negotiated pricing for large orgs
- API / export access

## Metrics

- Feature requests

## Risks and mitigations

- **Content quality risk**: inconsistent or shallow briefs. Mitigation: editorial process, SME review.
- **Regulatory news volume**: some months more than others. Mitigation: consistent structure; keep shorter when quiet.
- **Scope creep**: subscribers wanting custom consulting. Mitigation: clear product/service boundary; refer to Reseller-licensed consultants.
- **Competition**: law firms offer similar alerts. Mitigation: our angle is plain-language practical focus for operators, not BigLaw depth for lawyers.
