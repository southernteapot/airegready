# AI in Manufacturing & Industrial — Readiness Checklist

**Company:** ______________________________
**Completed by:** ______________________________
**Date:** ______________________________

---

## Phase 1 — Scope

- [ ] **1.1** Inventory AI in: products, factory-floor automation, predictive maintenance, QC, supply chain, design, office/ERP
- [ ] **1.2** Tagging: safety-critical / product-embedded / informational
- [ ] **1.3** EU AI Act Annex I / III classification documented
- [ ] **1.4** Export-controlled or dual-use flagged

## Phase 2 — Product safety

- [ ] **2.1** Functional safety analysis for AI in safety-critical roles (IEC 61508 / ISO 13849 or sector equivalents)
- [ ] **2.2** Hazard analysis includes AI failure modes (drift, adversarial, data-quality)
- [ ] **2.3** Post-market surveillance plan covers AI-related incidents
- [ ] **2.4** Change control process treats material AI updates as design changes
- [ ] **2.5** Duty-to-warn language in documentation

## Phase 3 — EU AI Act

- [ ] **3.1** Annex I sectoral legislation applied where AI is a safety component
- [ ] **3.2** Technical documentation (Annex IV) complete
- [ ] **3.3** QMS (Art 17), risk management (Art 9), data (Art 10), logs (Art 12), transparency (Art 13), oversight (Art 14), accuracy/security (Art 15)
- [ ] **3.4** Conformity assessment chosen and completed
- [ ] **3.5** CE marking and EU declaration (Art 47-48)
- [ ] **3.6** EU database registration
- [ ] **3.7** Art 73 serious incident reporting wired up

## Phase 4 — Workplace AI

- [ ] **4.1** No prohibited workplace emotion recognition (EU AI Act Art 5)
- [ ] **4.2** Employee / works council consultation where required
- [ ] **4.3** State workplace monitoring law compliance
- [ ] **4.4** Notice to workers before AI monitoring
- [ ] **4.5** False-positive remediation for safety-enforcement AI

## Phase 5 — Predictive maintenance and vendor data

- [ ] **5.1** Data flows mapped (what leaves the plant, to whom)
- [ ] **5.2** Vendor contracts address data ownership, training use, confidentiality
- [ ] **5.3** IP leakage risks identified and mitigated
- [ ] **5.4** Vendor model version control

## Phase 6 — OT cybersecurity

- [ ] **6.1** IEC 62443 / ISA-99 applied to AI-enabled OT
- [ ] **6.2** Segmentation between IT and OT networks
- [ ] **6.3** CISA critical-infrastructure expectations mapped (if in scope)
- [ ] **6.4** CIRCIA incident reporting configured
- [ ] **6.5** Backup and recovery for AI models

## Phase 7 — Supply chain

- [ ] **7.1** Supplier AI assessed for quality and compliance
- [ ] **7.2** Upstream AI model provenance documented
- [ ] **7.3** Alternative suppliers or fallback models

## Phase 8 — Design / engineering AI

- [ ] **8.1** IP attribution for AI-assisted designs
- [ ] **8.2** Quality controls for generative design outputs
- [ ] **8.3** Simulation validity testing before production

## Phase 9 — Environmental

- [ ] **9.1** Emissions / discharge AI meets regulator accuracy standards
- [ ] **9.2** No synthetic / AI-generated data in regulatory filings without disclosure and controls
- [ ] **9.3** Record-keeping meets environmental law

## Phase 10 — Export controls

- [ ] **10.1** Classification of AI models, training data, and documentation under EAR/ITAR
- [ ] **10.2** Export license determination for transfers
- [ ] **10.3** Deemed-export analysis for foreign personnel

## Phase 11 — Incident response

- [ ] **11.1** Plan covers AI malfunction in factory, product, or supply chain
- [ ] **11.2** Recall and consumer notice paths
- [ ] **11.3** Regulator reporting (CPSC, NHTSA, OSHA, EU market surveillance)

## Phase 12 — Records

- [ ] **12.1** Technical docs, validation, logs, incidents, corrective actions
- [ ] **12.2** Retention aligned to product safety regimes (often 10+ years)
