## The five rules (one page summary)

1. **A human is always responsible.** AI helps. People decide.
2. **Confidential stays confidential.** Don't paste customer, employee, or proprietary data into unapproved AI tools.
3. **Check the output.** AI sounds confident even when it's wrong. Verify before you send.
4. **Be honest about AI involvement.** Disclose where it matters.
5. **Follow the law.** When in doubt, ask.

## Traffic light for data

- 🟢 **Green**: public info → any approved tool
- 🟡 **Yellow**: internal business info → approved tools only
- 🔴 **Red**: customer data, employee records, health, financial, source code, contracts → only in tools specifically approved for that data class

## What you can do

- Research and summarize public information
- Draft and edit emails, memos, documents (review before sending)
- Help with code (review and test before shipping)
- Summarize internal meetings (with consent from attendees)
- Translate text
- Brainstorm ideas
- First-draft images for internal decks

## What you can't do

- Paste customer, employee, or confidential data into unapproved tools
- Make final hiring, firing, pay, or disciplinary decisions based on AI output alone
- Create deepfakes or voice clones of real people
- Give medical, legal, or financial advice to customers without qualified review
- Pretend AI-generated messages came from a human when the recipient expected a person
- Install unapproved AI tools on company devices

## Questions new hires often ask

### "Can I use ChatGPT on my personal account for work?"

No. Use approved tools via company SSO. If you have a good use case that our approved list doesn't cover, let us know and we'll review.

### "I pasted something I shouldn't have. What do I do?"

Tell us right away — [REPORTING CHANNEL]. We'd much rather deal with an honest quick report than discover it later. No one has been punished for a good-faith, promptly reported mistake.

### "Can I use AI to draft my performance self-review?"

Yes, with your own review. HR reviews these seriously; your real voice matters most.

### "Someone on my team is doing something I'm not sure is OK. What should I do?"

Mention it in [#ai-questions] if informal, or use [REPORTING CHANNEL] if you'd prefer. Non-retaliation is the rule.

### "How do I know if a tool is approved?"

Check the approved tools list at [LINK]. It's updated as we vet new tools.

### "I'm worried my job might be affected by AI."

Talk to your manager and HR. We're clear about our use of AI — it's to make the company's work faster and better, not to replace thoughtful people. We'll be direct with you about any changes that affect your role.
