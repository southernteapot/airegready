# AI at [COMPANY NAME] — One-Page Summary

**The short version of our AI Acceptable Use Policy. Read the full policy for details.**

---

## The five rules

1. **A human is always responsible.** AI helps. It doesn't decide.
2. **Confidential stays confidential.** Don't paste private data into unapproved tools.
3. **Check the output.** AI sounds confident even when it's wrong. Verify before you send.
4. **Be honest about how you worked.** Disclose AI assistance where it matters.
5. **Follow the law.** When in doubt, ask.

---

## What you can do with approved AI tools

- Research, summarize, brainstorm
- Draft and edit emails, memos, documents
- Help with code (then test it)
- Transcribe meetings (with everyone's consent)
- Generate first-draft images and copy

Always review AI output before it leaves the company.

---

## What you cannot do

- Paste customer, employee, health, financial, or other sensitive data into unapproved tools
- Use AI to make final hiring, firing, pay, or disciplinary decisions
- Create deepfakes or voice clones of real people
- Give medical, legal, or financial advice to customers without qualified human review
- Pretend AI-generated customer messages came from a human when the customer expected a real person
- Install unapproved AI tools on company devices

---

## The traffic light for data

- **Green** — public info. OK in any approved tool.
- **Yellow** — internal business info. OK in tools approved for internal use.
- **Red** — customer data, employee data, health, financial, source code, contracts. Only in tools explicitly approved for that data class.

When in doubt: **don't paste.** Ask [POLICY OWNER] first.

---

## How to get a new tool approved

Email [APPROVAL CONTACT] with:
- What tool you want to use
- What you'd use it for
- What data you'd put into it
- Who else on the team would use it

We'll run a short vendor review and add it to the approved list if it checks out.
