## AI Acceptable Use Policy

**Tagline:** Rules for how your team can use AI at work — in plain English.

**Summary:** A ready-to-adapt acceptable use policy that covers what's OK, what's not, and what to do when the rules get fuzzy. Written to be read by every employee, not just lawyers. Works for companies of 5 to 5,000.

**Who it's for:** Any business with employees using AI tools — which is almost everyone now.

**What's included:**
- Policy.md — the full 16-section policy
- Employee acknowledgment form
- One-page summary for distribution

**Why it's worth it:** An AUP is the foundation of every serious AI program. It reduces shadow-AI risk, creates a clear standard for your team, and supports "reasonable care" if a regulator asks. DIY-ing one takes weeks; this gets you there in hours.

**What you'll customize:** Company name, approved tool list, policy owner, reporting channels, review schedule.

## AI Vendor Risk Assessment

**Tagline:** Ten sections. Every question that matters before you sign.

**Summary:** A questionnaire and scoring guide for evaluating AI vendors before purchase or renewal. Covers data handling, security, bias, regulatory fit, and exit. Designed to be sent to vendors or filled from their trust centers.

**Who it's for:** Anyone procuring AI tools — IT, procurement, privacy, security, business owners.

**What's included:**
- Assessment.md — 10 sections, ~55 questions
- Scoring guide — how to turn answers into Low / Medium / High / Unacceptable

**Why it's worth it:** AI vendors vary wildly. A structured assessment catches the gaps a demo won't show. Saves you from discovering a month in that the vendor trains on your data by default.

**What you'll customize:** Your data classes, risk appetite, and required certifications.
