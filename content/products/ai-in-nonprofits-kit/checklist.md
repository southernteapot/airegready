# AI for Nonprofits — Lightweight Readiness Checklist

**Organization:** ______________________________
**Completed by:** ______________________________
**Date:** ______________________________

> Designed for small and mid-size nonprofits. Scales up for larger orgs. If any "yes" triggers heightened risk (beneficiary PII, hiring, eligibility decisions), escalate to the full operational templates.

---

## Phase 1 — Quick scan

- [ ] **1.1** One person is the AI point of contact
- [ ] **1.2** A one-page AI policy is published to staff
- [ ] **1.3** An approved tools list exists
- [ ] **1.4** All staff know how to report an AI concern
- [ ] **1.5** Board is aware AI is a topic

## Phase 2 — Data care

- [ ] **2.1** Beneficiary data is never pasted into consumer AI tools
- [ ] **2.2** Donor data is never pasted into consumer AI tools
- [ ] **2.3** Approved tools for beneficiary / donor data have signed data agreements
- [ ] **2.4** Especially sensitive populations (survivors of abuse, undocumented people, HIV+, etc.) have extra care procedures

## Phase 3 — Fundraising

- [ ] **3.1** AI-drafted donor communications reviewed for accuracy
- [ ] **3.2** No fake or AI-generated testimonials
- [ ] **3.3** No AI-generated "beneficiary" faces presented as real
- [ ] **3.4** CAN-SPAM / TCPA compliance for automated outreach

## Phase 4 — Grant writing

- [ ] **4.1** AI-assisted grants have a human-reviewer before submission
- [ ] **4.2** All numbers, citations, program claims verified
- [ ] **4.3** Transparent to grantors if they ask

## Phase 5 — Hiring

- [ ] **5.1** Any ATS / AEDT features on the approved tools list
- [ ] **5.2** NYC LL 144 / Illinois AIVIA / Colorado / California obligations checked
- [ ] **5.3** Candidate notice in postings
- [ ] **5.4** Accommodation process available

## Phase 6 — Program AI (beneficiary-facing)

- [ ] **6.1** Human-in-the-loop for eligibility / priority decisions
- [ ] **6.2** Accessibility (ADA) tested for beneficiary-facing interfaces
- [ ] **6.3** Safety guardrails on chatbots serving vulnerable populations
- [ ] **6.4** Opt-out path for beneficiaries who don't want AI
- [ ] **6.5** Sector-specific rules applied (HIPAA, FERPA, FHA)

## Phase 6 (alt) — Advocacy content

- [ ] **6.6** No deepfakes of real officials or members of public
- [ ] **6.7** Election-period obligations respected
- [ ] **6.8** Attribution / sourcing honest

## Phase 7 — Governance touch

- [ ] **7.1** AI review on quarterly ED check-in
- [ ] **7.2** Annual board update
- [ ] **7.3** Risk register includes AI item
- [ ] **7.4** Annual training refreshed
- [ ] **7.5** Policy reviewed annually

## Phase 8 — Records

- [ ] **8.1** Inventory updated quarterly
- [ ] **8.2** Data agreements filed
- [ ] **8.3** Incident log (even informal) maintained
