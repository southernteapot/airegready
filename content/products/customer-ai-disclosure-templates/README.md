# Customer AI Disclosure Templates

Short, ready-to-adapt disclosure language for the places customers or the public encounter AI at [COMPANY NAME]. Tailor each snippet to your product, tone, and legal review before using.

## Why you need these

Multiple laws now require you to tell people when they are interacting with AI, when content is AI-generated, or when AI is involved in a decision that affects them. These include:

- **EU AI Act Art 50** — chatbots, emotion recognition, biometric categorization, deepfakes
- **Colorado AI Act** — notice before a high-risk AI system is a substantial factor in a consequential decision; adverse-action explanation
- **California** (various): TFAIA, AB 2013, SB 942, and others
- **Texas TRAIGA** — state-agency disclosure requirements; certain private-sector requirements
- **NYC Local Law 144** — automated employment decision tools
- **Illinois AIVIA** — AI in video interviews
- **Utah AI Policy Act** — disclosure for regulated occupations
- **FTC** — unfair / deceptive-practice risk if AI interactions are disguised

Disclosure is also good practice even where not strictly required. It builds trust and reduces complaints.

## How to use this folder

- `website-disclosure.md` — language for a general "How we use AI" page
- `chatbot-disclaimer.md` — opening message for an AI-powered chat or voice assistant
- `email-signature.md` — short line for email signatures or automated messages
- `terms-addendum.md` — contract-grade addendum for terms of service / master subscription agreements
- `hiring-notice.md` — notice to candidates where AI is used in hiring
- `adverse-action.md` — notice for adverse decisions produced or assisted by AI

Every snippet uses `[BRACKETED]` placeholders. Have Legal review before publishing.

## General rules of thumb

1. **Say it plainly.** "You are chatting with an AI" beats "our conversational AI agent."
2. **Say it early.** Before the interaction, not buried in a privacy policy.
3. **Say it specifically.** Name the AI's role (answering questions, screening, scoring). Vague mentions don't satisfy most laws.
4. **Offer a human path** where it matters. Customer trust — and some laws — depend on it.
5. **Document what you said and when.** Screenshots, versioned copy, deployment dates.
