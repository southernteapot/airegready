# How We Use AI at [COMPANY NAME]

*Last updated: [DATE]*

At [COMPANY NAME], we use artificial intelligence ("AI") to make our work faster, clearer, and more accurate. Because AI is still a new technology with real limits, we also want to be upfront about where and how it shows up in our products.

## Where AI is involved

### [USE CASE 1 — e.g., Customer support chat]

When you message us through our website chat, you may first interact with an AI assistant that answers common questions. If the AI can't help, or if you ask to speak to a person, you'll be connected to a human team member. Conversations are logged and reviewed for quality.

### [USE CASE 2 — e.g., Product recommendations]

We use AI to suggest products you may like based on what you've browsed or purchased. These recommendations are suggestions only. They don't change the price you're offered or the terms of any purchase.

### [USE CASE 3 — e.g., Fraud prevention]

We use AI tools to help detect fraudulent transactions. A human reviews any account flagged by the system before we take action.

### [USE CASE 4 — e.g., Content generation]

Some of the written or visual content on our site is produced with the help of AI drafting tools. A human reviewer edits and approves anything we publish. Where AI generated material that a reader should know is AI-made, we label it clearly.

## What we don't do

- We don't use AI to make final decisions in [CONTEXTS YOU EXCLUDE — e.g., hiring, lending, medical recommendations].
- We don't sell your interactions with our AI to third parties.
- We don't use your data to train AI models without permission. *[Adjust to match your actual practice.]*

## How we oversee our AI

- Every AI system we use is reviewed by our AI Review Committee before deployment and at least annually afterward.
- We use an AI risk management program aligned to [NIST AI Risk Management Framework / ISO/IEC 42001 / other].
- We monitor our AI systems for errors, bias, and unexpected outputs, and we correct or retire systems that fall short.
- If we discover that an AI system has harmed someone, we notify the people affected and the appropriate authorities as required by law.

- **To know** when AI is used in a decision about you
- **To correct** the personal information used by the system
- **To appeal** an adverse decision to a human reviewer, where feasible
- **To opt out** of certain automated processing
