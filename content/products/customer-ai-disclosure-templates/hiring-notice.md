# Hiring — AI Disclosure to Candidates

Notice language for candidates when AI is used in hiring. Written for contexts covered by NYC Local Law 144, Illinois AIVIA, Colorado AI Act, California regulations, EU AI Act (Annex III employment), and similar rules. Have HR and legal review.

---

## Short form — careers page / job posting

> **How we use AI in our hiring process.** [COMPANY NAME] uses AI-assisted tools to help review applications and schedule interviews. Every hiring decision is made by a person, not by an AI system. If you'd like to opt out of AI-assisted review, or request accommodations, email [CAREERS EMAIL] before you apply. Learn more at [HOW-WE-USE-AI PAGE].

## Long form — candidate notice (sent before assessment)

> **AI in our hiring process — what you should know**
>
> Dear [CANDIDATE NAME],
>
> As part of our hiring process for the [POSITION TITLE] role, we may use AI-assisted tools. We want to be transparent about this before you continue.
>
> **What we use AI for**
>
> - Reviewing submitted resumes to help our recruiters prioritize applications
> - [Conducting an automated video interview where your responses are analyzed for relevant skills]
> - [Scoring responses on a skills assessment]
> - Scheduling interviews and follow-ups
>
> **What AI does not do**
>
> - No hiring decision is made solely by AI. A qualified recruiter or hiring manager reviews candidates before any decision is made.
> - We do not use AI to make inferences about your race, ethnicity, religion, disability, or other protected characteristics.
>
> **Data we collect and how we use it**
>
> We collect the information you provide in your application and, where applicable, your responses to assessments or video interviews. This information is used only for the hiring process for this role [or for consideration for other open roles, if you consent]. It is retained for [RETENTION PERIOD] and then deleted.
>
> **Your rights**
>
> - You may request human-only review of your application instead of AI-assisted review. Email [CAREERS EMAIL] at least [3 business days] before any assessment.
> - You may request an accommodation for a disability or other reason at any stage of the process.
> - You may request that we correct information about you that you believe is inaccurate.
> - Where a decision is adverse to you and AI meaningfully contributed, you may request an explanation of the principal factors.
>
> **Who to contact**
>
> For questions about how AI is used in our hiring process, contact [HR CONTACT NAME] at [EMAIL].
>
> By continuing with your application, you confirm that you've read this notice.
>
> Thank you for applying,
> [HR TEAM]

## Short form — Illinois AIVIA (video interview)

> **Notice about AI in your video interview.** The video interview for this role will be analyzed by an AI system to help evaluate your responses. Before you proceed: (1) we are notifying you that AI will be used; (2) we are providing an explanation of how the AI works and what it evaluates [LINK or attach]; and (3) we need your consent to use AI analysis. If you prefer not to consent, please let us know at [EMAIL] and we will arrange a human-only interview instead.

## Short form — adverse action for hiring (AI-assisted rejection)

> Dear [CANDIDATE NAME],
>
> Thank you for your interest in the [POSITION TITLE] role at [COMPANY NAME]. After careful review, we won't be moving forward with your application at this time.
>
> We want to be open with you: an AI-assisted tool was used in screening applications for this role. The principal factors considered were [BRIEF DESCRIPTION — e.g., experience with listed technologies, relevant role history, alignment with required qualifications]. The final decision was made by [HIRING MANAGER / RECRUITER].
>
> If you believe information about you that was used in this process is inaccurate, you may request correction by emailing [HR EMAIL]. If you would like to request human review of this decision, please reply to this email within [14] days.
>
> We wish you the best in your search,
> [HR CONTACT]
