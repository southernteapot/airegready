# Email / Automated Message — AI Disclosure

Short lines for adding to email signatures, auto-responders, or AI-drafted customer messages.

---

## For messages AI drafted and a human reviewed

> *[Optional footer]* This message was prepared with the help of AI drafting tools and reviewed by a member of our team before sending.

## For messages sent by an AI without human review (e.g., automated replies)

> *This is an automated message sent by an AI assistant on behalf of [COMPANY NAME]. For a personal reply from our team, please reply "agent" or email [HUMAN EMAIL].*

## Signature block for human team members who use AI drafting assistants

No footer needed in most cases. Only add a disclosure if:

- The message commits [COMPANY NAME] to anything material (contracts, promises, commitments)
- The message contains advice that needs to be true (legal, financial, medical, safety)
- The recipient has a reasonable expectation that the writer wrote every word themselves
- A sector rule requires disclosure (some professional contexts)

Suggested line for those cases:

> *[NAME] — drafted with AI assistance; reviewed and approved before sending.*

## Out-of-office auto-reply using AI

> *Thanks for your message. I'm out of office until [DATE]. While I'm away, an AI assistant may send an initial reply to point you to the right resources. For anything urgent, please contact [BACKUP CONTACT]. I'll follow up personally when I return.*

## Sales outreach via AI

> *This outreach was prepared with AI assistance. If you'd prefer not to hear from us, reply "unsubscribe" and we'll remove your address.*

## What to avoid

- Don't hide AI authorship in messages where authorship matters (condolences, apologies, offers, letters of recommendation)
- Don't let an AI auto-respond to sensitive topics (complaints, health concerns, legal threats) without a handoff
- Don't include disclosure language that's so small or buried that a reasonable person wouldn't see it
