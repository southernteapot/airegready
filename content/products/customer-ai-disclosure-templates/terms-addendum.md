# Terms of Service / Master Agreement — AI Addendum

Contract-grade language that can be added to a Terms of Service, Master Subscription Agreement, or Data Processing Agreement to address AI-specific matters. Have counsel review before using. Tailor to match your actual practice — misaligned contracts are worse than no contract.

---

## Definitions

**"AI Features"** means the components of the Service that use machine learning or generative artificial intelligence to produce outputs, including without limitation text, images, audio, video, code, predictions, scores, or recommendations.

**"Input"** means data, text, files, prompts, or other material submitted by Customer or its Authorized Users to the AI Features.

**"Output"** means any material generated by the AI Features in response to Input.

**"Training"** means the use of data to develop, fine-tune, or improve a machine-learning model.

## 1. License and ownership

1.1 As between the parties, Customer retains all rights, title, and interest in and to Input, subject to the licenses granted herein.

1.2 Subject to Customer's payment of fees and compliance with this Agreement, [COMPANY NAME] grants Customer a non-exclusive, non-transferable, worldwide license to use Output for its internal business purposes and as otherwise described in the Documentation.

1.3 Output may not be unique and may be similar to outputs generated for other customers. Customer is solely responsible for reviewing Output before relying on it.

## 2. Inputs and training

**Option B (opt-in training):** [COMPANY NAME] may use aggregated and de-identified usage metadata to improve the Service. [COMPANY NAME] will not use Customer Input or Output for Training except where Customer expressly opts in.

**Option C (default training with opt-out):** Customer may opt out of having Input or Output used for Training by [MECHANISM — e.g., changing account settings, emailing privacy@[COMPANY]].

## 3. Accuracy and human review

3.1 AI Features can produce inaccurate, incomplete, or inappropriate Output. Output may appear confident while being wrong.

3.2 Customer is solely responsible for reviewing Output before using it, distributing it, or acting on it.

3.3 Customer will not rely on Output as professional advice (including legal, medical, financial, or tax advice) and will not represent Output as [COMPANY NAME]'s advice.

## 4. Prohibited uses

Customer will not use AI Features to:

(a) generate or distribute unlawful, infringing, defamatory, harassing, or deceptive content;
(b) make final decisions affecting a natural person's access to or terms of employment, lending, housing, insurance, healthcare, education, government services, or legal services without qualified human review, or in violation of applicable law;
(c) create synthetic media of an identifiable person without that person's consent;
(d) process special categories of personal data (including health, biometric, or children's data) outside the scope expressly authorized by the Agreement;
(e) reverse engineer the AI Features, extract weights, or attempt to derive the underlying model;
(f) use Output to train a competing AI system;
(g) violate the Acceptable Use Policy at [LINK].

## 5. Data protection and security

5.1 [COMPANY NAME]'s processing of personal data in connection with the AI Features is governed by the Data Processing Agreement at [LINK], which is incorporated by reference.

5.2 [COMPANY NAME] maintains security controls described in [SECURITY ADDENDUM LINK] applicable to the AI Features.

5.3 Customer is responsible for ensuring that any Input it provides complies with applicable law and that Customer has the right to provide such Input.

## 6. Transparency and disclosure obligations

6.1 Where required by applicable law, Customer will inform end users and data subjects of the use of AI Features, including the purpose of the AI Features and any decisions being made with the assistance of AI.

6.2 [COMPANY NAME] will provide reasonable assistance to Customer in meeting Customer's transparency obligations under applicable AI regulation (including but not limited to EU AI Act, Colorado AI Act, California TFAIA, and Texas TRAIGA), consistent with [COMPANY NAME]'s role as [provider / processor].

## 7. Changes to the AI Features

7.1 [COMPANY NAME] may update, modify, or replace the underlying models and AI Features from time to time. [COMPANY NAME] will provide at least [NN] days' notice of material changes that are reasonably likely to materially affect Customer's use of the Service.

7.2 Material changes include model deprecations, material changes to safety behavior, and new categories of Input required.

## 8. Intellectual property and indemnity

8.1 [COMPANY NAME] will defend Customer against any third-party claim that Output, as delivered by [COMPANY NAME] and used by Customer in accordance with the Documentation, infringes a third party's intellectual property rights, and will pay any damages finally awarded or agreed to in settlement of such claim, subject to [STANDARD INDEMNITY LIMITS AND EXCEPTIONS].

8.2 The indemnity does not apply to infringement caused by Customer's Input, Customer's modifications, or Customer's combination of Output with other material.

## 9. Incident cooperation

9.1 Each party will cooperate reasonably in connection with any AI-related incident that may trigger notification or reporting obligations under applicable law, including incidents involving personal data, discriminatory output, or serious malfunction.

9.2 The parties acknowledge that the EU AI Act (Art 73) and analogous U.S. laws impose reporting obligations with short deadlines and will coordinate to meet those timelines.

## 10. Regulatory compliance

10.1 [COMPANY NAME] represents that, as of the Effective Date, it complies in all material respects with applicable AI-related laws in the jurisdictions where the AI Features are made available.

10.2 Customer represents that its use of the AI Features will comply with applicable law.

10.3 If either party determines in good faith that continued operation of the AI Features would violate applicable law, that party may suspend use, with notice, until compliance is restored.

## 11. Survival

The obligations in Sections 3 (Accuracy), 4 (Prohibited Uses), 5 (Data Protection), 8 (IP and Indemnity), 9 (Incident Cooperation), and 10 (Regulatory Compliance) survive termination of the Agreement.
