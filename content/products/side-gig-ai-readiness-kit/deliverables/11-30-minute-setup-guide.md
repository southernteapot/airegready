# 30-Minute Setup Guide

Purpose: Show the shortest practical path through the Solo Builder AI Launch Kit when you need a useful first version quickly.

## When To Use It

Use this when you are close to launching, publishing, demoing, selling, or sending AI-assisted work and need a first working record today.

This is the minimum viable version, not the full review path.

## How To Fill It Out

Set a 30-minute timer. Write rough but honest answers. Use Unknown, Needs review, or Not applicable where needed. Do not stop to polish language unless it will be user-facing.

## 0-5 Minutes: AI Project Snapshot

Complete only these fields:

- Project name
- Owner
- Date
- Stage
- What the project does
- Who uses it
- Who pays for it
- Where AI is used
- AI model/vendor/tool
- User data involved
- Outputs produced
- Main risks or unknowns
- Next review date

Minimum viable answer:

If a field is not clear, write one sentence about what you still need to decide.

## 5-10 Minutes: AI Claims Checklist

Find the 3-5 most important claims from your landing page, product page, demo, email, social post, or sales script.

For each claim, ask:

- Is it specific?
- Is it true today?
- Can I support it?
- Could a user hear it as a guarantee?
- Does it imply legal, financial, medical, professional, safety, compliance, or business-outcome advice?

Minimum viable answer:

Rewrite or remove the one claim that makes you most uncomfortable.

## 10-15 Minutes: User Data And Privacy Intake

List:

- What users type or upload
- What you paste into AI tools
- What goes to third-party AI vendors
- What logs or outputs are saved
- What sensitive data you want users to avoid submitting
- Whether users are told AI is involved

Minimum viable answer:

Write one clear sentence: This AI workflow uses [DATA] to produce [OUTPUT], and users should not submit [DATA TO AVOID].

## 15-20 Minutes: Red Flag Use Case Triage

Scan the red-flag list and mark Yes, No, or Unsure for:

- health
- mental health
- legal
- financial
- credit
- employment
- housing
- education
- insurance
- children or minors
- biometrics
- identity verification
- surveillance
- synthetic media
- high-stakes decisions
- sensitive personal data
- scraping or third-party data
- automated decisions with real-world consequences

Minimum viable answer:

If anything is Yes or Unsure, write one next step: pause, narrow scope, add human review, get qualified advice, or document the decision.

## 20-25 Minutes: Disclosure Builder

Draft three short notes:

1. AI-use disclosure: This feature uses AI to [TASK].
2. Output limitation: AI output may be incomplete or incorrect, so review it before use.
3. Report-a-problem path: Contact [EMAIL OR FORM] if something looks wrong.

Minimum viable answer:

Place the disclosure where users will see it before relying on output.

## 25-30 Minutes: Pre-Launch Checklist And Change Log

Complete the final launch decision in the Pre-Launch AI Risk Checklist.

Then add the first AI Change Log entry:

| Date | Change type | What changed | Why it changed | Next review |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [DATE] | Launch baseline | First version of AI project reviewed with Solo Builder AI Launch Kit. | Prepare before public or paid launch. | [DATE] |

Minimum viable answer:

Choose one final decision:

- Ready to launch as scoped
- Launch only after listed fixes
- Narrow scope before launch
- Pause and get qualified help
- Do not launch in current form

## What Good Enough Looks Like

A good 30-minute version should make these things visible:

- what the AI does
- what data it touches
- what claims you are making
- what users should know
- what red flags might exist
- what changed or needs review next

It does not need to sound polished. It needs to be specific enough that future you can understand what you decided.

## Review Carefully If

- You marked any red flag as Yes or Unsure.
- You are launching a paid offer before finishing the full review path.
- You do not yet have a user-facing disclosure or report-a-problem path.
- You found a claim that sounds like a guarantee, compliance promise, professional advice, or certain business outcome.

Short boundary: This worksheet is educational information, not legal advice. It is a starting point for documenting your AI project and spotting questions to review. Specific legal, privacy, contractual, or regulatory obligations should be reviewed with qualified counsel or the appropriate advisor.
