# AI Claims Checklist

Purpose: Review what you say your AI can do before users, customers, clients, platforms, or reviewers see the claim.

## When To Use It

Use this before publishing a landing page, product page, demo, ad, email sequence, social post, pitch deck, sales script, onboarding screen, FAQ, marketplace listing, or client proposal.

Use it again when you change the AI feature, model, prompt, workflow, target customer, or public positioning.

## How To Fill It Out

Paste or summarize the claim. Mark whether it is factual, supported, vague, overstated, or needs review. Rewrite risky claims into narrower, more specific language.

## Sections To Review

### Accuracy Claims

- [ ] Do you say the AI is accurate, precise, reliable, or error-free?
- [ ] Do you explain what the AI is good at and where it can be wrong?
- [ ] Do you have test results, examples, or review notes that support the claim?
- [ ] Does the claim avoid implying professional certainty where there is none?

### Automation Claims

- [ ] Do you say the AI fully automates a task?
- [ ] Is a human still reviewing, editing, approving, or applying judgment?
- [ ] Does the copy explain what remains the user's responsibility?
- [ ] Does the workflow fail safely if the AI gives a weak or incomplete output?

### Earnings Or Business Outcome Claims

- [ ] Do you say the AI saves money, increases revenue, gets clients, improves conversion, or guarantees a result?
- [ ] Is the claim based on real evidence or only hope, anecdotes, or examples?
- [ ] Does the copy avoid promising results that depend on the user's market, skill, budget, or execution?

### Professional Advice Claims

- [ ] Could a user think the AI is giving legal, medical, mental health, financial, tax, HR, safety, or other professional advice?
- [ ] Does the page say what the tool is and is not for?
- [ ] Is there a clear path to qualified human review where needed?

### Safe, Compliant, Guaranteed, Or Human-Level Claims

- [ ] Do you use words like safe, compliant, guaranteed, certified, approved, human-level, expert-level, risk-free, or fully automated?
- [ ] If so, can you support the exact claim?
- [ ] If not, rewrite it into a narrower claim about what the tool helps the user do.

## Rewrite Risky Claims Into Safer Claims

| Risky claim pattern | Safer direction |
| --- | --- |
| This makes your AI compliant. | This helps you document key AI project facts and spot questions to review. |
| Protects you from liability. | Helps you keep a practical record of claims, data use, disclosures, and changes. |
| Legally safe AI launch. | A plain-English starting point for preparing your AI project before launch. |
| Guaranteed accurate output. | Draft output that should be reviewed before use. |
| Fully automates customer support. | Drafts suggested replies that a person can review before sending. |
| Human-level expert advice. | Provides general AI-generated suggestions that may need qualified review. |
| All you need for compliance. | A lightweight documentation kit, not a substitute for legal or regulatory review. |

## Review Carefully If

- The claim involves compliance, safety, certification, approval, professional advice, earnings, accuracy, or real-world outcomes.
- The claim appears in paid ads, marketplace listings, sales pages, investor materials, or client contracts.
- The AI output could affect a person's rights, money, health, job, education, housing, insurance, or access to services.

Short boundary: This worksheet is educational information, not legal advice. It is a starting point for documenting your AI project and spotting questions to review. Specific legal, privacy, contractual, or regulatory obligations should be reviewed with qualified counsel or the appropriate advisor.
