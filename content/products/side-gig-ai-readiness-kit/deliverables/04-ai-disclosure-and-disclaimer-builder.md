# AI Disclosure and Disclaimer Builder

Purpose: Help you draft plain-English starter language that tells users where AI is involved, what the output can and cannot do, and how to report problems.

## When To Use It

Use this when AI output appears in an app, website, automation, client deliverable, support flow, digital product, or paid offer. It can support landing pages, onboarding screens, FAQs, client proposals, product notes, support pages, or feature labels.

## How To Fill It Out

Choose the modules that fit your project, edit them into your own voice, and review them against the actual product behavior. These examples are starting language, not final legal terms.

Replace bracketed text with your own details. Remove any statement that is not true for your project.

## Modular Starter Language

### AI-Use Disclosure

Use when users should know that AI is part of the feature or workflow.

Starter option:

This feature uses AI to [summarize / draft / classify / suggest / generate] [TYPE OF OUTPUT]. AI helps produce the first pass, but the output may be incomplete, outdated, or incorrect.

Short option:

This feature uses AI to help generate [TYPE OF OUTPUT]. Please review the result before relying on it.

Client-work option:

I may use AI tools to help organize notes, draft outlines, summarize intake material, or prepare first-pass work. I review AI-assisted work before sending it to you.

### Output Limitation

Use when output may be wrong, incomplete, or context-dependent.

Starter option:

AI-generated output can be useful, but it may miss context, misunderstand inputs, or produce incorrect details. You are responsible for reviewing the output before using it in decisions, publications, customer messages, or client work.

### Human Review

Use when a person reviews output before it is sent, published, or used.

Starter option:

AI output is reviewed by [ME / OUR TEAM / THE USER] before [it is sent / it is published / a final decision is made]. The AI does not make final decisions on its own.

If there is no human review, say that plainly and narrow the use case:

This feature generates automated suggestions. They are not reviewed before display, so users should treat them as starting points and check important details independently.

### Not Professional Advice

Use when a user might mistake general output for legal, medical, financial, tax, mental health, HR, safety, or other professional advice.

Starter option:

This tool provides general AI-generated information and drafting support. It is not professional advice and should not be used as a substitute for qualified advice about your specific situation.

### User Responsibility

Use when users control the final use of the output.

Starter option:

You decide whether and how to use the output. Please check important facts, adapt the result to your situation, and avoid submitting private, sensitive, or confidential information unless the product instructions say it is appropriate.

### Report A Problem

Use when users need a practical contact path.

Starter option:

If you see an incorrect, harmful, confusing, or unexpected AI output, contact [SUPPORT EMAIL OR FORM] with a short description of what happened and, if possible, a screenshot or example.

### Data-Use Note

Use when user data enters an AI workflow.

Starter option:

The AI feature processes [TYPE OF DATA] to produce [TYPE OF OUTPUT]. Do not submit [TYPES OF DATA TO AVOID]. For questions about data use, contact [SUPPORT EMAIL OR FORM].

### Experimental Or Beta Feature Note

Use when the AI feature is still being tested.

Starter option:

This AI feature is in beta. It may change, produce unexpected results, or work better for some inputs than others. Please review the output carefully and report issues to [SUPPORT EMAIL OR FORM].

## Placement Checklist

- [ ] The disclosure appears before or near the AI feature.
- [ ] The disclosure uses plain language.
- [ ] It says what the AI does.
- [ ] It says what the AI does not do.
- [ ] It explains whether a human reviews the output.
- [ ] It tells users what not to submit, if relevant.
- [ ] It gives users a way to report a problem.
- [ ] It does not promise compliance, safety, approval, or guaranteed results.
- [ ] It has been reviewed against the actual product behavior.

## Review Carefully If

- Users may rely on the output for important decisions.
- The AI touches sensitive data, client confidential information, or regulated subject matter.
- The disclosure appears inside legal terms, privacy notices, contracts, professional advice contexts, or paid ads.
- You are unsure whether the disclosure matches how the AI feature really works.

Short boundary: This worksheet is educational information, not legal advice. It is a starting point for documenting your AI project and spotting questions to review. Specific legal, privacy, contractual, or regulatory obligations should be reviewed with qualified counsel or the appropriate advisor.
