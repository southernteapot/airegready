# AI Change Log

Purpose: Track changes to the model, prompt, vendor, data use, claims, disclosures, and product behavior over time.

## When To Use It

Use this whenever you change anything that could affect what the AI does, what data it touches, what users see, what you claim, or what records you rely on.

## How To Fill It Out

Add one row per meaningful change. Small typo fixes may not need a row. Anything that affects output behavior, data flow, vendor dependency, public copy, disclosure, customer FAQ, or review scope should be logged.

## Change Types

- Model change
- Vendor change
- Prompt change
- System instruction change
- Data-use change
- Logging or analytics change
- Feature behavior change
- Public claim change
- Disclosure or FAQ change
- Human review change
- Red-flag scope change
- Incident-driven change
- Other

## Review Prompts

Before a change goes live, ask:

- Does this change what users should be told?
- Does this change what data goes into the AI workflow?
- Does this change the accuracy, tone, risk, or reliability of output?
- Does this change whether human review is needed?
- Does this make any public claim outdated?
- Does this create a new red-flag category?
- Does this require a customer FAQ update?
- Does this require qualified review?

## Review Carefully If

- You changed the vendor, model, prompt, system instruction, data source, or logging behavior.
- You changed public claims, pricing pages, demos, or onboarding copy.
- You removed human review or made output more automated.
- The change was made because of a complaint, bad output, privacy concern, or customer confusion.
