# User Data and Privacy Intake

Purpose: Map what user, customer, client, or audience data touches your AI workflow before you launch or change it.

## When To Use It

Use this when your AI project collects form responses, uploads, account data, support messages, client materials, analytics, chat logs, prompts, files, or other information from people or businesses.

Use it before adding a new AI vendor, prompt, automation, file upload, logging feature, analytics tool, or customer-facing workflow.

## How To Fill It Out

List what data enters the workflow, where it goes, who can see it, and what you should avoid. Do not use this worksheet to decide legal conclusions. Use it to prepare better questions and records.

## Review Carefully If

- You send user data, client data, confidential files, or sensitive categories to a third-party AI vendor.
- Children or minors may use the product or appear in the data.
- The AI output affects people outside your own internal workflow.
- Logs, analytics, support tools, or error reports may capture prompts, files, or outputs.
- You are unsure whether users have been told AI is involved.

Short boundary: This worksheet is educational information, not legal advice. It is a starting point for documenting your AI project and spotting questions to review. Specific legal, privacy, contractual, or regulatory obligations should be reviewed with qualified counsel or the appropriate advisor.
