## Short Product Summary

A plain-English launch-readiness kit for solo builders using AI in apps, websites, automations, consulting offers, digital products, or AI-powered services. It helps you document what AI is doing, what data goes in, what claims you are making, what users should know, what red flags exist, and what changed over time.

## Long Product Description

You built the thing. Now make sure your AI claims, data use, user disclosures, and change records are not scattered or invisible.

The Solo Builder AI Launch Kit is for one-person businesses and small commercial projects that use AI in a real offer: an app, website, automation, digital product, consulting workflow, client deliverable, productized service, or AI-powered tool. It gives you a practical starting file before you publish, sell, demo, advertise, or take on customers.

The kit helps you write down what your AI feature or workflow does, what data it uses, which vendors or models it depends on, what your public copy says, what users should be told, what obvious red flags need attention, and what changed after launch. It is intentionally lighter than an organization-wide governance program. The goal is not to make you compliant or remove risk. The goal is to keep your AI use, claims, disclosures, and changes visible enough that you can spot obvious gaps, ask better questions, and prepare before launch.

Use it when you are still moving fast, but your AI project is commercial enough that memory, notes, and scattered chat threads are no longer enough.

## Who It Is For

This kit is best for:

- solo founders and one-person businesses using AI commercially
- indie hackers and micro-SaaS creators adding AI features
- freelancers, consultants, and productized-service sellers using AI in client work
- no-code builders, app builders, and website builders launching AI-enabled offers
- AI automation sellers building workflows for customers or clients
- digital product creators selling templates, guides, prompts, courses, or tools
- side gig builders who want a simple pre-launch record before they sell, publish, advertise, or take clients

It is especially useful when you have built an AI-enabled offer and do not want the claims, data use, disclosures, or risk notes to stay scattered across drafts, tools, chats, and memory.

## Who It Is Not For

This kit is not the right fit if you need:

- formal legal, privacy, security, HR, procurement, or compliance review
- a compliance guarantee, certification, audit, or regulator-ready opinion
- a substitute for qualified counsel or other professional advice
- organization-wide AI governance for employees, departments, approval workflows, or enterprise customers
- full vendor due diligence for a major AI provider or procurement process
- regulated-use review for hiring, lending, housing, insurance, healthcare, education, legal, financial, public-sector, or other high-impact decisions
- a claim that using this kit will prevent legal trouble, protect you from liability, or make your AI product compliant

For broader internal AI rules and governance records, use the AI Governance Starter Kit. For deeper vendor review, use a vendor review packet or qualified procurement, privacy, security, or legal support.

## What Is Included

### AI Project Snapshot

Helps you capture the basic facts about your AI-enabled product, service, workflow, or launch.
Why it matters: a simple snapshot keeps the project from living only in your head, codebase, prompt history, or sales copy.
Question it answers: What am I launching, where does AI show up, and who is affected by it?

### AI Claims Checklist

Helps you review what your landing page, product page, emails, demos, ads, and sales conversations say your AI can do.
Why it matters: unsupported or vague AI claims can create avoidable risk and customer confusion.
Question it answers: What exactly am I promising users this AI will do?

### User Data and Privacy Intake

Helps you list what user, customer, client, or audience data enters the AI workflow and what should stay out.
Why it matters: solo builders often move fast, and data-use decisions can become invisible unless they are written down.
Question it answers: What data goes into the AI system, and what data should I avoid collecting, pasting, uploading, or storing?

### AI Disclosure and Disclaimer Builder

Helps you draft plain-English notes about when AI is used, what users should understand, and where disclaimers may be needed.
Why it matters: users, clients, and buyers should not have to guess where AI is involved or what limits apply.
Question it answers: What should I tell users or customers about the role and limits of AI in this offer?

### Red Flag Use Case Triage

Helps you sort obvious lower-risk, needs-review, and avoid-or-escalate AI use cases before launch.
Why it matters: not every AI use deserves the same level of concern, but some uses need more than a casual checklist.
Question it answers: Is this AI use simple enough to proceed, or does it need qualified review before I sell or publish it?

### Vendor / Model Dependency Note

Helps you record the AI tools, models, APIs, plugins, agents, datasets, or platforms your launch depends on.
Why it matters: vendor terms, model behavior, pricing, uptime, and feature changes can affect what you sell and support.
Question it answers: Which outside AI systems am I relying on, and what could change if they change?

### Pre-Launch AI Risk Checklist

Helps you run a practical final check before publishing, advertising, demoing, onboarding users, or taking client work.
Why it matters: a short pre-launch review catches obvious gaps while there is still time to fix the page, workflow, or disclosure.
Question it answers: What should I check before this AI offer goes live?

### Customer FAQ Template

Helps you prepare answers to common customer, client, or user questions about your AI use.
Why it matters: buyers may ask what the AI does, what data it uses, whether outputs are reviewed, and what limits apply.
Question it answers: How will I answer basic AI trust questions without improvising every time?

### Incident / Complaint Mini-Log

Helps you record complaints, unexpected AI behavior, user confusion, bad outputs, data concerns, and follow-up actions.
Why it matters: small issues are easy to lose in email, chat, support messages, or memory, especially when you are the whole team.
Question it answers: What happened, who raised it, what did I do, and what should change next?

### AI Change Log

Helps you track model changes, prompt changes, feature changes, disclosure updates, claim edits, and risk-note updates over time.
Why it matters: AI launches keep moving, and yesterday's review may not match today's product or workflow.
Question it answers: What changed since the last time I reviewed this AI project?

### 30-Minute Setup Guide

Helps you create a first working file quickly, without trying to build a full governance program.
Why it matters: solo builders need a practical starting point they can actually finish before launch.
Question it answers: What should I fill out first if I only have half an hour?

### Filled Example: AI Website or App

Shows what the kit can look like for an AI-enabled website, app, wrapper, no-code tool, or micro-SaaS feature.
Why it matters: examples make the templates easier to use and show the right level of detail.
Question it answers: How would this look for an AI product users interact with directly?

### Filled Example: Consultant or Freelancer Using AI

Shows how a solo service provider can document AI use in client research, drafting, analysis, automation, or deliverables.
Why it matters: client-facing AI use raises practical questions about disclosure, data, review, and scope.
Question it answers: How should I think about AI when it helps me produce client work?

### Filled Example: Digital Product, Automation, or Micro-SaaS

Shows how the kit can apply to a template, paid guide, workflow automation, prompt product, lightweight SaaS, or AI-powered digital offer.
Why it matters: digital products and automations can blur the line between content, tool, service, and software.
Question it answers: How do I document an AI-assisted offer that is small but still commercial?

## Why It Helps

The Solo Builder AI Launch Kit helps by turning scattered AI decisions into a simple launch file. It gives you a place to document what AI is doing, what data is involved, what claims need support, what users should know, which uses deserve review, and what changed after launch.

It helps you:

- spot obvious gaps before a customer, client, platform, or reviewer spots them for you
- ask better questions of legal, privacy, security, platform, or client reviewers when those reviewers are needed
- keep AI use, claims, and changes from becoming invisible as the project evolves
- prepare clearer customer-facing answers without overpromising
- create a lightweight record that matches the reality of a solo commercial project

It does not make the project compliant, remove legal obligations, approve a regulated use case, or protect you from liability.

## How It Differs From the AI Governance Starter Kit

**Solo Builder AI Launch Kit** is best for one-person external or commercial AI projects. It focuses on launch claims, user data, disclosures, red flags, customer questions, model and vendor notes, incident notes, and change logs.

**AI Governance Starter Kit** is best for founders, new businesses, consultants, operators, and lean teams that need broader AI governance basics, including tool inventory, acceptable use rules, risk intake, team rollout, approval records, and governance records.

Use the Solo Builder AI Launch Kit when the immediate question is: "Can I explain and document this AI-powered offer before I put it in front of users, customers, or clients?"

Use the AI Governance Starter Kit when the immediate question is: "How do we create basic internal rules and records for AI use across a business or team?"
