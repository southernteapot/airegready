# Utah AI Policy Act — Readiness Kit Overview

**Regulation:** Utah Artificial Intelligence Policy Act (S.B. 149, 2024, codified at Utah Code Title 13, Chapter 72)
**Effective:** May 1, 2024 (initial provisions); subsequent amendments have expanded coverage
**Enforcement:** Utah Division of Consumer Protection (DCP); and the Utah Office of Artificial Intelligence Policy

> **How to use this kit.** For businesses interacting with Utah consumers using generative AI, regulated occupations using AI, and companies participating in Utah's regulatory sandbox. Pair with the AI Acceptable Use Policy, Customer AI Disclosure Templates, and AI Governance Framework.

---

## 1. Why Utah matters

Utah was one of the first U.S. states to pass a broad, private-sector-facing AI law. It has three noteworthy elements:

1. **Disclosure** obligations for generative AI interacting with consumers, especially in regulated occupations
2. A **regulatory sandbox** program administered by the Office of AI Policy (the "Learning Laboratory") that lets companies test AI under limited liability
3. **Accountability clarity** — using AI does not shield you from existing consumer protection laws

Utah has been an early mover on AI-specific consumer protection enforcement, including actions against companies making misleading AI-related claims.

## 2. Key obligations

### 2.1 Disclosure in regulated occupations

When a person regulated by the Utah Division of Occupational and Professional Licensing (DOPL) uses generative AI to provide services within the scope of the occupation, the person must:

- **Prominently disclose** to the consumer that the consumer is being provided services by a generative AI tool
- Disclosure must be clear and conspicuous at the start of the interaction

Covered professions include medical, mental health, legal, and other licensed practices. The specific list has expanded; verify current DOPL coverage.

### 2.2 Consumer disclosure generally

Where a consumer asks whether they are interacting with AI, the person / business must clearly acknowledge.

Subsequent amendments have clarified and, in some cases, narrowed the initial broader reading. Monitor current language.

### 2.3 Accountability

Utah makes explicit that using generative AI does not insulate a person from liability for misrepresentations, deceptive practices, or statutory violations. The AI cannot be the "fall guy."

### 2.4 Regulatory sandbox — the Learning Laboratory

Administered by the Utah Office of AI Policy. Provides:

- A structured path to test innovative AI applications
- Limited liability / enforcement discretion during participation
- Reporting obligations back to the Office
- Typically time-boxed and scope-limited

Useful if you're building something on the edge — voice cloning, therapy chatbots, automated legal services — that would otherwise carry unclear regulatory risk.

## 3. Who is covered

- **Regulated occupations** operating in Utah — providers of medical, legal, mental health, financial, and other DOPL-covered services
- **Consumer-facing businesses** using generative AI that touches Utah residents
- **AI operators** broadly — anyone whose generative AI system interacts with a Utah consumer in a way that triggers disclosure expectations

## 4. Practical readiness steps

1. **Identify uses** — every place generative AI interacts with Utah consumers
2. **Determine regulated-occupation status** — if yes, comply with prominent disclosure requirements at the start of service
3. **Update chatbot and voice-agent disclosures** — front-load AI identity
4. **Update website AI disclosures** — include Utah-specific language where used in regulated services
5. **Train staff** on responding to "am I talking to a bot?" honestly
6. **Prepare evidence** that AI output has human oversight where appropriate
7. **Consider sandbox** if you're building edge-of-regulation AI
8. **Monitor enforcement** — Utah DCP has been active

## 5. Enforcement posture

Utah DCP has pursued enforcement actions against companies:

- Making misleading claims about AI capabilities
- Failing to disclose AI use in regulated contexts
- Using AI to mask unlawful practices

Penalties can include fines per violation and injunctive relief. Deceptive-practice doctrine applies.

## 6. Interaction with other regimes

- **Utah Consumer Sales Practices Act** — continues to apply; AI doesn't create exemptions
- **Federal FTC** — similar disclosure and accountability expectations
- **Colorado AI Act** — if you serve both CO and UT consumers, satisfy both
- **EU AI Act** — for EU exposure
- **Healthcare, legal, financial** — sector rules apply alongside AIPA

## 7. Common traps

- **Assuming "AI did it" is a defense** — it's not
- **Buried disclosures** — Utah expects prominence
- **Missing regulated-occupation obligations** — specific rules apply when licensed professionals use AI in their practice
- **Not monitoring AG posture** — enforcement has been active
