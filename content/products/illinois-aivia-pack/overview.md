# Illinois AI Video Interview Act (AIVIA) — Compliance Pack

**Regulation:** Illinois Artificial Intelligence Video Interview Act (820 ILCS 42)
**Enforcement:** Illinois Department of Labor; private right of action considerations
**Related Illinois laws:** Illinois Human Rights Act (as amended to address AI in employment decisions, effective January 1, 2026); Biometric Information Privacy Act (BIPA)

> **How to use this pack.** Required reading for any employer using AI to analyze video interviews of candidates for Illinois-based positions or Illinois residents. Pair with the AI in HR / Hiring Pack, Customer AI Disclosure Templates, and AI Vendor Risk Assessment.

---

## 1. What AIVIA requires

Illinois was the first state with AI-in-hiring law. AIVIA applies when an employer uses an AI system to analyze a **video interview** of an applicant for a position based in Illinois.

Three core obligations:

1. **Notice** before the interview
2. **Explanation** of how the AI works and what characteristics it evaluates
3. **Consent** from the applicant to the AI's use

Plus downstream rules on **sharing**, **destruction**, and **demographic-reporting** (which applies where the AI is used as a significant factor in hiring decisions).

## 2. Notice, explanation, consent

Before the interview:

- **Notify** the applicant that AI may be used to analyze the video
- **Explain** how the AI works and the general types of characteristics the AI uses to evaluate candidates
- **Obtain consent** from the applicant to be evaluated by the AI

If the applicant does not consent, the employer must provide an alternative evaluation path (typically a human-only interview).

## 3. Sharing restrictions

Employers may not share applicant videos except with persons whose expertise or technology is necessary to evaluate the applicant. The typical reading: interviewers, HR, the AI vendor, and necessary service providers — not broader.

## 4. Destruction

Upon applicant request, the employer must:

- **Destroy** all copies of the applicant's videos within **30 days**
- Ensure the destruction includes copies held by all recipients and vendors

## 5. Demographic reporting

For employers that rely **solely** on AI analysis to determine whether the applicant will be selected for an in-person interview, annual reporting obligations apply to the Illinois Department of Commerce and Economic Opportunity:

- Race / ethnicity of applicants
- Race / ethnicity of those offered in-person interviews
- Race / ethnicity of those hired

If your AI is used for multiple purposes or if human review is part of the funnel, document the role of AI vs. human carefully.

## 6. Illinois HRA — AI in employment (effective 2026)

Illinois amended the Human Rights Act (HB 3773, effective January 1, 2026) to address AI in employment decisions. Key points:

- Prohibits employers from using AI that has a **discriminatory effect** on protected classes
- Prohibits employers from using **ZIP code** as a proxy for race in AI employment decisions
- Requires **notice** to employees or applicants when AI is used in employment decisions

Combined with AIVIA: Illinois employers using AI in hiring / employment have layered obligations.

## 7. Interaction with BIPA

Illinois BIPA regulates biometric identifiers:

- **Face geometry scans** (which can be extracted from video) are biometric identifiers under BIPA
- BIPA requires written notice and consent before collecting biometrics
- BIPA has a **private right of action** with statutory damages

If your video interview AI performs face-geometry analysis, BIPA applies in addition to AIVIA. BIPA damages have driven large class actions.

## 8. Best practice: structure your video interview AI

To minimize risk:

- Prefer AI that analyzes **content** (responses to questions, relevant skills indicators) over AI that analyzes **facial expressions, micro-expressions, or voice emotion**
- Avoid emotion inference in the workplace / hiring (prohibited by EU AI Act; contentious under Title VII / ADA / state law)
- Use human interviewers as the primary decision-makers; AI as assistance, not substitute
- Document the features evaluated; avoid protected-class proxies
- Offer a reasonable non-AI alternative

## 9. Notice language

See `notice.md` for a ready-to-adapt template. Highlights:

- Clear description of what AI does
- Plain-language explanation of characteristics evaluated
- Consent option
- Alternative path if applicant declines
- Contact for questions and destruction requests

## 10. Destruction requests

Build a workflow:

- Easy channel for applicants to request destruction
- Intake within 1 business day
- Destruction in all systems within 30 days
- Vendor coordination (they hold copies)
- Confirmation back to applicant

## 11. Practical readiness steps

1. Confirm whether any AI analyzes videos for Illinois-based positions or Illinois applicants
2. Update notice and consent process
3. Build / document destruction workflow
4. If solely-AI filter, set up demographic reporting
5. Review vendor contracts for flow-down
6. Train recruiters and hiring managers
7. Coordinate with BIPA compliance (if face-geometry analysis)
8. Prepare for HRA (2026) — broader employment-AI notice

## 12. Common traps

- **Using face / expression analysis** without realizing it triggers BIPA
- **Burying notice** in a lengthy consent blob
- **No alternative process** when applicant declines
- **No destruction workflow** — applicant requests lost in mail
- **Missing demographic reporting** for solely-AI filters
- **Assuming AIVIA is all that applies** — HRA + BIPA add layers
