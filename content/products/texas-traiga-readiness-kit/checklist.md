## Phase 1 — Scope

- [ ] **1.1** Confirmed [COMPANY NAME] does business in Texas or reaches Texas consumers / employees / state agencies
- [ ] **1.2** AI System Inventory updated with Texas-exposure tag
- [ ] **1.3** Identified systems that serve Texas state agencies (contract pass-through obligations)
- [ ] **1.4** Identified systems that process biometric data of Texans
- [ ] **1.5** Identified systems that generate synthetic content (deepfakes, generated media)

## Phase 2 — Prohibited use review

For each AI system in scope:

- [ ] **2.1** Assessed whether the system could reasonably be characterized as **manipulative** (bypassing reasoned decision-making to cause physical or financial harm)
- [ ] **2.2** Assessed whether the system could reasonably be characterized as **exploitative** (targeting vulnerabilities based on age, disability, or socioeconomic status)
- [ ] **2.3** Assessed whether the system could produce **intentional discriminatory outcomes** against protected classes
- [ ] **2.4** Assessed whether the system could produce, transmit, or enable CSAM or non-consensual intimate imagery (and, if any adjacency, implemented guardrails)
- [ ] **2.5** Assessed whether the system performs **social scoring** (and, if applicable, confirmed the system is not operated by or for a state agency in a prohibited way)
- [ ] **2.6** Assessed whether the system performs **emotion inference or biometric categorization** in protected contexts
- [ ] **2.7** For each "no" conclusion above, documented the basis clearly (intent, safeguards, guardrails)

## Phase 3 — Consumer disclosure

- [ ] **3.1** Every chatbot and voice agent interacting with Texas consumers presents a **clear and conspicuous** AI disclosure at or before the point of interaction
- [ ] **3.2** Disclosure language reviewed against Texas "reasonable consumer" standard
- [ ] **3.3** Automated communications (email, SMS, push) identify the AI sender or origin where not obvious
- [ ] **3.4** Synthetic content (audio, image, video) carries labeling as required by statute; machine-readable markings where feasible
- [ ] **3.5** Handoff messaging ("connecting you to a person now") configured
- [ ] **3.6** "Are you human?" responses configured to answer honestly

## Phase 4 — Biometric provisions

If you process any biometric identifiers of Texans:

- [ ] **4.1** Mapped biometric identifiers (face, voice, iris, fingerprint, gait, etc.) to purpose and legal basis
- [ ] **4.2** Consent captured as required (written or equivalent) before collection
- [ ] **4.3** No untargeted scraping of biometric data from the internet or public recordings used to build or train identification systems
- [ ] **4.4** Vendor contracts audited for biometric-pass-through obligations
- [ ] **4.5** Retention limits set (default per CUBI: destroy within a reasonable period, generally no later than one year after the purpose is fulfilled)
- [ ] **4.6** Deletion-on-request process in place
- [ ] **4.7** Disclosures to employees (if biometric used in workplace) documented

## Phase 5 — Government sector (if applicable)

If you are or serve a Texas state agency:

- [ ] **5.1** Public AI inventory maintained and current
- [ ] **5.2** Disclosures to consumers of agency AI systems implemented
- [ ] **5.3** Agency AI impact review completed
- [ ] **5.4** No agency use of AI for social scoring or prohibited categories
- [ ] **5.5** Vendor contracts include TRAIGA flow-down obligations

## Phase 6 — Synthetic content pipelines

- [ ] **6.1** Content generation products label outputs as AI-generated as required
- [ ] **6.2** Machine-readable markings (watermarks, provenance metadata) applied where technically feasible
- [ ] **6.3** Deepfake use cases reviewed for consent and likeness rights; identifiable-person outputs restricted without consent
- [ ] **6.4** Non-consensual intimate imagery detection/prevention in place for platforms that could host user-generated synthetic media
- [ ] **6.5** Policies against CSAM generation documented and enforced

## Phase 7 — Governance and documentation

- [ ] **7.1** AI Governance Framework references TRAIGA prohibited categories explicitly
- [ ] **7.2** Internal policy prohibits development for purposes listed in TRAIGA
- [ ] **7.3** Training rolled out to product, engineering, marketing, and sales teams
- [ ] **7.4** Procurement reviews new vendors for TRAIGA alignment
- [ ] **7.5** Customer contracts updated to reflect prohibited-use limitations
- [ ] **7.6** Employee agreements updated to prohibit using company AI tools for TRAIGA-prohibited purposes

## Phase 8 — Complaint intake and monitoring

- [ ] **8.1** Channel for consumer complaints about AI behavior established
- [ ] **8.2** Internal reporting path for employees who detect prohibited-use risk
- [ ] **8.3** Pattern detection for complaints suggesting manipulation, discrimination, or impersonation
- [ ] **8.4** Escalation to Legal on AG inquiries or notices
- [ ] **8.5** Cure-period process documented — if AG notice is received, a rapid remediation team can engage

## Phase 9 — Sandbox (optional)

If considering the sandbox:

- [ ] **9.1** Use case evaluated against sandbox eligibility
- [ ] **9.2** Sandbox application drafted with DIR
- [ ] **9.3** Reporting and safety commitments documented
- [ ] **9.4** Legal review completed

## Phase 10 — Records and audit readiness

- [ ] **10.1** TRAIGA policy, inventory, and impact materials maintained in a retrievable location
- [ ] **10.2** Evidence of training, consent capture, disclosure text, and monitoring artifacts retained
- [ ] **10.3** Retention period set (at least [3 years], verify with counsel)
