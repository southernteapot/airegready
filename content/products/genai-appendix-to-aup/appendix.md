## 1. Scope

This appendix covers use of **generative AI** tools — tools that produce text, code, images, audio, video, or structured content. It applies to everyone using these tools for work at [COMPANY NAME].

## 2. Key additional rules

### 2.1 Always treat output as a draft

- AI output is a draft until a competent human reviews it
- Never send, publish, or file AI output without a human review appropriate to the stakes
- "Draft" obligations apply to text, code, images, video, and audio

### 2.2 Verify facts, citations, and references

- Generative AI fabricates plausibly
- Check names, dates, numbers, quotes, case citations, URLs, statute references, and claims of fact before relying on them
- "AI said so" is never a source

### 2.3 Disclosure when it matters

Disclose generative AI assistance:

- In public-facing creative work where authorship matters (editorials, creative work, executive statements)
- In customer communications where the customer has a reasonable expectation of human authorship
- In hiring, performance, and consequential decisions about people
- Where law or professional ethics require (courts, regulators, bar associations)

### 2.4 Don't pass off as original

- Don't submit AI-generated work as your own in contexts where original authorship is required (e.g., certifications, exams, academic credits)
- Don't claim AI-generated art or copy as a distinctive human creation without disclosure

### 2.5 Respect IP in inputs

- Don't use copyrighted material as an input (for prompting / fine-tuning / training) without a license basis or a sound fair-use analysis
- Don't use employee or customer material beyond the scope agreed upon
- Don't submit third-party confidential information for generation

### 2.6 Respect IP in outputs

- Review outputs for likely IP issues (copyrighted images, identifiable trademarks, distinctive artist styles without license)
- Don't use outputs that substantially reproduce specific copyrighted works
- Document review where high-risk

### 2.7 Respect real persons

- No AI-generated voices or images of real people without written consent
- No deepfakes in marketing or internal use
- Extra caution with public figures, minors, deceased persons

### 2.8 Don't generate prohibited content

You may not generate or attempt to generate:

- CSAM or non-consensual intimate imagery
- Targeted harassment
- Content that incites violence or unlawful acts
- Material falsely attributed to real persons
- Content that infringes third-party IP
- Election / political disinformation
- Professional advice (legal, medical, financial) for publication without qualified review
- Content using protected-class characteristics to disadvantage people

### 2.9 Brand voice and accuracy

- AI-drafted content in [COMPANY NAME]'s voice must be reviewed for brand alignment
- Marketing content must be substantiated (see AI Marketing Claims Review)
- Regulatory claims must be cleared by Legal before publishing

### 2.10 Data handling — gen AI specifics

- Prompts and retrieved context count as data transfers
- Don't paste confidential data into public gen AI tools
- Approved tools have DPA / no-training commitments; use them
- Enterprise / team versions are usually required for anything beyond public info

## 3. Tool-class guidance

### 3.1 Text and code generation

- Cite original sources when the AI pulled from specific material
- Code reviews apply to AI-generated code — don't ship untested code
- Security-review AI code for vulnerabilities; gen AI is a fertile source of insecure patterns
- Don't commit AI-generated secrets, tokens, or test credentials

### 3.2 Image generation

- No depiction of real people without consent (see Synthetic Media Policy)
- No style-copy of identifiable living artists without analysis
- No trademarked logos or brands without clearance
- Watermark / metadata preservation where required

### 3.3 Voice synthesis

- No voice cloning of real people without consent (Tennessee ELVIS Act, state right of publicity)
- AI voice in robocalls / marketing subject to state AI voice laws and TCPA
- Disclose AI voice on customer calls

### 3.4 Video / deepfake

- No deepfakes in production except as expressly authorized with consent
- Disclosures required by EU AI Act Art 50, California SB 942, Texas TRAIGA, state election laws

### 3.5 Agents / autonomous systems

See the **Agentic AI Policy** for specific rules.

## 4. Gen AI-specific incidents

Flag and report:

- Hallucinated citations, quotes, or facts in published material
- Inadvertent PII disclosure in prompt or output logs
- IP concerns raised by a rights-holder
- Deepfake complaints from a depicted individual
- Discrimination concerns from AI-generated content
- Vendor changes that materially affect output behavior

## 5. Enforcement

Violations may result in verbal or written warnings, revocation of AI tool access, formal discipline up to termination, and contractual / legal consequences.
