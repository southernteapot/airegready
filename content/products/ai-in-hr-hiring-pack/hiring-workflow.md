# Hiring Workflow with AI Compliance Checkpoints

A workflow that wires compliance requirements into the standard hiring process. Adapt the stages to your actual hiring funnel. Every box below becomes a task in your ATS or an HR SOP.

---

## Stage 0 — Program setup (before hiring season)

- [ ] AI System Inventory includes every AI hiring tool in use
- [ ] Bias audits current for in-scope tools (NYC LL 144 within 12 months)
- [ ] Public audit summary posted on careers page
- [ ] Notice language integrated into job posting template
- [ ] Adverse-action notice template current
- [ ] Accommodation / alternative selection process staffed and trained
- [ ] Recruiter and hiring-manager training complete for the year
- [ ] HR Addendum to AI AUP acknowledged by all hiring personnel

## Stage 1 — Requisition and job posting

For each new requisition:

- [ ] Role and qualifications reviewed for job-relatedness and non-discriminatory language
- [ ] Decision: which AI tools will be used in this hiring process?
- [ ] Post includes the applicable **Candidate Notice** (10-business-day lead for LL 144; appropriate for other jurisdictions)
- [ ] Post links to the **Bias Audit Summary** on the careers page
- [ ] Post includes accommodation / alternative process contact

## Stage 2 — Sourcing

- [ ] Sourcing AI tools used only within approved scope
- [ ] No queries inferring protected characteristics
- [ ] Outreach communications include bot disclosures where candidates are contacted by automated systems
- [ ] Outside recruiters and agencies briefed on the hiring AI addendum

## Stage 3 — Application received

For every application received:

- [ ] Acknowledgment email includes AI notice (even if the job posting already did — belt and suspenders for NYC LL 144 and CA ADMT)
- [ ] Application data stored per retention policy
- [ ] Demographic data captured where lawful and separated from decisioning inputs
- [ ] If the candidate requests accommodation or alternative selection, flag the record and route to HR

## Stage 4 — AEDT / AI screening

Before running the AEDT on a batch:

- [ ] Confirm all candidates had the required notice lead time
- [ ] Process any pending accommodation / alternative selection requests first
- [ ] Confirm tool version has a current bias audit
- [ ] Run the AEDT; record input, output, and tool version per candidate

After running:

- [ ] Human reviewer examines outputs for anomalies
- [ ] Reviewer decision recorded separately from AEDT output
- [ ] Alternative selection candidates reviewed by a qualified recruiter
- [ ] Any candidate rejected at this stage receives an **Adverse Action Notice** if AEDT was a material factor

## Stage 5 — Assessments and video interviews

- [ ] If a video interview AI analyzer is used: comply with Illinois AIVIA
  - Notify the candidate before the interview
  - Provide explanation of how the AI works
  - Obtain consent (and support declined consent with a non-AI alternative)
  - Do not share the video beyond the decision-makers and required storage
  - Destroy the video within 30 days on request
- [ ] If scored assessments are used:
  - Scored only by approved tools with current bias audits
  - Results reviewed by a human decision-maker alongside other evidence
- [ ] Accommodation requests honored (extended time, alternative format, human evaluation)

## Stage 6 — Interview

- [ ] Structured interview guide followed
- [ ] No AI emotion inference / facial analysis in use
- [ ] AI-transcribed notes, if used, have all-participant consent and are stored per retention policy
- [ ] Interviewer biases addressed (unconscious bias training, diverse panels where possible)

## Stage 7 — Reference / background check

- [ ] Background check vendor is FCRA-compliant; pre-adverse action notice process in place
- [ ] If AI-scored background check is used, document the scoring rationale
- [ ] Candidate rights under FCRA honored (dispute rights; access to report)

## Stage 8 — Offer

- [ ] Decision-maker documents principal reasons for selection in plain language
- [ ] Offer communications comply with applicable state notice rules (salary transparency, location, etc.)
- [ ] Signing candidate acknowledges AI Acceptable Use Policy as part of onboarding

## Stage 9 — Rejection

For every candidate who reaches the rejection stage after an AI-assisted review:

- [ ] Adverse-Action Notice sent with:
  - Principal reasons for the decision (specific, plain language)
  - Data sources used
  - Right to correct personal data
  - Right to appeal to a human reviewer within [14 days]
  - FCRA disclosures where applicable
- [ ] Appeal path (if requested) routed to a qualified human reviewer within SLA
- [ ] Records retained per policy

## Stage 10 — Post-hire

- [ ] New hire receives AI AUP with HR Addendum and signs the acknowledgment
- [ ] Employee's manager briefed on any AI tools used in their performance management (if any)
- [ ] First 90-day feedback includes a signal on candidate experience with AI in the process (used as a bias and process quality input)

---

## Ongoing

### Weekly

- Review AEDT outputs for anomalies (extreme rejection rates, unusual patterns)
- Review accommodation / alternative selection requests handled

### Monthly

- Review complaint volume and categories
- Spot-check adverse-action notices for quality and timeliness
- Confirm audit summary on careers page is current

### Quarterly

- Present HR AI posture to AI Review Committee
- Inventory shadow AI risks (agencies, third parties)
- Review any adverse-action appeals and patterns

### Annually

- Re-run bias audits for all in-scope AEDTs (earlier if material modifications)
- Republish summaries on careers page
- Refresh training for recruiters and hiring managers
- Review vendor contracts at renewal for AI-specific terms

### Ad hoc

- On any regulatory change (new state law, EEOC update, DCWP enforcement action), review program
- On any candidate complaint suggesting bias or non-disclosure, escalate to Legal
