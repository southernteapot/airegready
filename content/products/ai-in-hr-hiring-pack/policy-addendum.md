> **How to use.** This addendum supplements the AI Acceptable Use Policy with rules specific to the use of AI in people-decisions: hiring, performance, promotion, discipline, and termination. It applies to everyone involved in those decisions — recruiters, hiring managers, HR, and their leaders.

---

## 1. Why this addendum exists

AI tools can speed up and improve people-decisions. They can also introduce bias at scale, violate privacy, and expose [COMPANY NAME] to legal risk under federal, state, and international law. This addendum sets rules so the benefits don't come at the cost of fairness or compliance.

## 2. Where this addendum applies

- Any use of AI in recruiting, sourcing, screening, assessing, or hiring
- Any use of AI in performance management, promotion, discipline, compensation, or termination decisions
- Any use of AI to monitor employees or contractors
- Any use of AI to analyze employee communications, sentiment, or productivity

## 3. Guiding principles

1. **Decisions about people are made by people.** AI can assist. A qualified human must be accountable for every employment decision.
2. **Explainable inputs.** If an AI output influenced a decision, a reviewer must be able to state the principal reasons in plain language.
3. **Equal access to opportunity.** Accommodations and alternative processes must be genuinely available, not technically.
4. **Notice to candidates and employees.** People affected by AI deserve to know.
5. **Audits, not assumptions.** Bias audits are planned and documented; we don't assume fairness.

## 4. Permitted uses

With approval per this addendum, AI may be used to:

- Draft job descriptions and postings (with human review)
- Source candidates from public sources (without discriminating inputs)
- Deduplicate applicant records
- Schedule interviews
- Translate communications
- Summarize interview notes (with participant consent)
- Screen resumes against job-stated qualifications (subject to bias audit and disclosure requirements)
- Score structured assessments (subject to bias audit and disclosure requirements)
- Support performance conversations with aggregated data (not auto-scoring)

## 5. Prohibited uses

AI may **not** be used to:

- Make final decisions about hiring, firing, promotion, pay, or discipline without qualified human review
- Infer protected characteristics (race, religion, disability, pregnancy, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, age) about candidates or employees
- Analyze candidate appearance, micro-expressions, or voice tone in a way that correlates with protected classes (see Illinois AIVIA and EEOC guidance)
- Monitor employees' emotional state or mood in the workplace (EU AI Act Art 5 prohibition; analogous state rules)
- Evaluate candidates using signals unrelated to the job (social media sentiment, political speech, etc.)
- Disqualify candidates with disabilities without an accommodation process
- Bypass [COMPANY NAME]'s anti-discrimination policies, Title VII, ADA, ADEA, NYC Human Rights Law, or any applicable equivalent

- [ ] Candidate notice language drafted and reviewed by Legal

- [ ] Public summary prepared for careers page where required
- [ ] Recruiters and hiring managers trained
- [ ] Inventory entry created and tier assigned

## 7. During use

- **Notice**: Every candidate must receive the applicable notice at least [10 business days] before the AI tool is used (NYC LL 144 where applicable; similar windows for other jurisdictions).
- **Human review**: No candidate is rejected or advanced solely on AI output. A qualified recruiter or hiring manager records a reason for each meaningful decision.
- **Accommodation**: Requests for accommodation or human-only review are processed without delay and never count against the candidate.
- **Records**: Inputs, outputs, decisions, and notices are logged to support bias audits and regulatory inquiries.
- **Complaints**: Any candidate complaint about AI in the process is routed to HR within [3 business days].

## 8. Adverse decisions

When a decision adverse to a candidate or employee is made and AI materially contributed:

- The affected person receives written notice that AI was used
- The notice states the principal reasons
- Data sources used are disclosed in categories the person can understand
- The person has the right to correct inaccurate data
- The person has the right to appeal to a human reviewer, and that right is honored within a documented SLA
- For Colorado, an internal 90-day clock runs for AG reporting if algorithmic discrimination is observed

## 9. Recruiters and hiring managers — responsibilities

- Know which tools are approved for your role, and use only those
- Watch for shadow AI use by agencies, partners, and outside recruiters — flag to HR leadership
- Attend annual training
- Report concerns, unusual patterns, or suspected bias to [HR LEAD]
- Preserve records of hiring-related correspondence

## 10. Monitoring AI use in the workplace

Using AI to monitor employees requires [EXECUTIVE SPONSOR + LEGAL] approval. Any workplace monitoring must:

- Be proportionate to a legitimate purpose
- Respect applicable privacy and consultation law (including EU works councils and U.S. state monitoring laws)
- Be disclosed to employees
- Not use emotion inference in the workplace where prohibited (EU AI Act Art 5)

## 11. Vendor and agency use

External recruiters, staffing agencies, and assessment vendors acting on behalf of [COMPANY NAME]:

- Must comply with this addendum
- Must disclose AI tools in use
- Must support bias audits and consumer-request processing
- Must allow [COMPANY NAME] to meet its notice and recordkeeping obligations

Contracts with these parties must reflect the above.

## 12. Review and training

- This addendum is reviewed annually and when regulations change materially
- All HR staff, recruiters, and hiring managers complete annual training and acknowledge the addendum
- Training includes: NYC LL 144 mechanics, Illinois AIVIA, Colorado AI Act employment provisions, EEOC guidance, and [COMPANY NAME]'s complaint and accommodation processes
