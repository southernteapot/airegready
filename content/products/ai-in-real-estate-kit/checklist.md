## Phase 1 — Scope

- [ ] **1.1** AI System Inventory covers leasing, tenant screening, mortgage, valuation, pricing, advertising, smart-home
- [ ] **1.2** Each system flagged for FHA, ECOA, FCRA, HMDA, CFPB/HUD AVM rule, state tenant-screening law, CAIA

## Phase 2 — Fair housing

- [ ] **2.1** Disparate-impact testing on screening, pricing, valuation, steering-prone recommendation systems
- [ ] **2.2** Protected-class proxy analysis (ZIP, surname, school district, etc.)
- [ ] **2.3** Less-discriminatory-alternative analysis documented where material impact identified
- [ ] **2.4** Results reviewed by legal and business owner
- [ ] **2.5** Periodic re-testing (annual minimum)

## Phase 3 — Tenant screening

- [ ] **3.1** FCRA compliance: pre-adverse and adverse-action notices with specific reasons
- [ ] **3.2** Appeal / dispute channel for adverse decisions
- [ ] **3.3** Criminal-history screening is individualized and proportionate (HUD guidance)
- [ ] **3.4** State-specific restrictions applied (CA, NY, WA, OR, etc.)
- [ ] **3.5** Source-of-income protections where applicable
- [ ] **3.6** Score explainability available

## Phase 4 — Advertising

- [ ] **4.1** Housing ads use platform-provided fair-housing settings (where required)
- [ ] **4.2** Lookalike audiences avoid protected-class exclusion
- [ ] **4.3** Ad copy reviewed for discriminatory preferences
- [ ] **4.4** Delivery monitoring for disparate outcomes

## Phase 5 — AVMs / valuation

- [ ] **5.1** AVM used in accordance with CFPB/HUD interagency quality-control rule
- [ ] **5.2** Bias testing by neighborhood / race / other protected classes
- [ ] **5.3** Human review path for outlier valuations
- [ ] **5.4** Documentation of AVM limitations for each use case

## Phase 6 — Rent pricing / revenue management

- [ ] **6.1** Antitrust review of revenue management system if participating in data-sharing
- [ ] **6.2** Pricing outputs reviewed for discriminatory patterns
- [ ] **6.3** Tenant notice where required

## Phase 7 — Leasing AI and chatbots

- [ ] **7.1** Chatbot identity disclosed
- [ ] **7.2** Fair-housing training for AI chatbot prompts and responses
- [ ] **7.3** No protected-class questioning
- [ ] **7.4** Accessibility tested for leasing portals

## Phase 8 — Mortgage AI (if applicable)

- [ ] **8.1** See AI in Financial Services Kit, Phase 5 (fair lending) and Phase 6 (data governance) in particular
- [ ] **8.2** ECOA adverse-action notices with specific reasons
- [ ] **8.3** HMDA reporting aligned with model use

## Phase 9 — Accessibility (ADA)

- [ ] **9.1** AI leasing agents accessible (screen readers, captions for voice)
- [ ] **9.2** Smart-home features support assistive technology
- [ ] **9.3** Alternative channels for users who can't use AI interfaces

## Phase 10 — Colorado AI Act

- [ ] **10.1** Impact assessments for high-risk housing AI
- [ ] **10.2** Consumer notice
- [ ] **10.3** Adverse-decision explanation and appeal
- [ ] **10.4** Public website statement
- [ ] **10.5** 90-day AG reporting process

## Phase 11 — Data governance

- [ ] **11.1** FCRA-related data handled consistent with FCRA
- [ ] **11.2** Tenant / borrower personal data handled per GLBA and state privacy law
- [ ] **11.3** Training data for custom models documented and license-cleared

## Phase 12 — Records

- [ ] **12.1** Screening decisions with inputs, outputs, and reasons retained per FCRA/state
- [ ] **12.2** AVM validation and testing retained
- [ ] **12.3** Fair-housing test results retained
- [ ] **12.4** Complaints and AG inquiries logged
