# NYC Local Law 144 — AEDT Compliance Pack

**Regulation:** NYC Administrative Code § 20-870 et seq. (Local Law 144 of 2021)
**Implementing rules:** NYC Department of Consumer and Worker Protection (DCWP) final rules effective 2023
**Enforcement:** DCWP; civil penalties per violation with each day of continued violation counted separately

> **How to use this pack.** Read the overview. Use `bias-audit-brief.md` to understand what goes in a compliant bias audit (and what to ask your auditor for). Use `candidate-notice.md` for ready-to-adapt candidate notice language. Pair with the AI Acceptable Use Policy, AI in HR / Hiring Pack, and AI Governance Framework.

## 1. Why this matters

NYC Local Law 144 is the country's first fully operational AI-in-hiring law with real enforcement. If you use an **Automated Employment Decision Tool (AEDT)** to screen or score candidates for jobs based in New York City, **or to screen NYC residents for employment**, the law applies to you — even if you're not based in New York.

Non-compliance isn't theoretical: DCWP has brought enforcement actions, and candidates and journalists have used the public audit summary requirement to surface patterns of non-disclosure.

## 2. Key terms

### AEDT — Automated Employment Decision Tool

Any computational process derived from machine learning, statistical modeling, data analytics, or AI that issues a simplified output (including a score, classification, or recommendation) used **to substantially assist or replace** discretionary decisionmaking for employment decisions.

- "Substantially assist or replace" includes: relying solely on the output; weighting the output more than other factors; using the output to overrule or modify a human decision.
- Tools that merely **assist** a human recruiter (e.g., scheduling, light deduplication) generally aren't AEDTs.

### Employment decision

Screening candidates for employment or promoting employees.

### Candidate for employment

A person who has applied for a specific position by submitting the necessary materials in the format required by the employer.

### Employee

An individual employed by an employer in NYC.

## 3. Key obligations

1. **Bias audit** — an annual independent bias audit of the AEDT before it's used
2. **Public summary** — a summary of the audit results published on the employer's website
3. **Distribution date** — date of most recent audit and the distribution date of the AEDT published alongside
4. **Candidate notice** — at least **10 business days before** using the AEDT, notify candidates/employees that an AEDT will be used, the job qualifications and characteristics the AEDT will use, and information about how to request accommodations / alternative process
5. **Data retention** — retain certain data to support audits

Failing any one of these is a violation. All four are required.

## 4. The bias audit — what it must include

The DCWP rules specify calculation methods. Summary:

- **Selection rate** or **scoring rate** for each sex category, race/ethnicity category, and intersectional categories of sex × race/ethnicity
- **Impact ratio** comparing selection / scoring rate of each category to the reference group
- **Number of applicants / employees** in each category, including unknown
- Description of the audit scope, data sources, methodology, and the date of the audit
- The audit must be performed by an **independent auditor** (no conflict of interest — not the employer, not the vendor, not a party that was involved in developing or operating the AEDT)

Historical data may be used; if insufficient, test data can be used, subject to disclosure.

## 5. The public summary — what must be posted

- Date of the most recent audit
- Distribution date of the AEDT
- A summary of the bias audit results
- Selection rate / impact ratio by sex, race/ethnicity, and intersectional categories
- Link to a detailed audit or the detailed audit itself

The summary must be **on the section of the employer's website that describes the employment selection procedure** (typically the careers page) or linked from there.

## 6. The candidate notice — what it must include

**10 business days or more before using the AEDT:**

- A notice that the AEDT will be used in the employment decision
- Job qualifications and characteristics that the AEDT will use
- Information about the type of data collected and source of such data
- The retention policy for the data
- Information on how to request an accommodation or an alternative selection process

Notice can be delivered in the job posting, by email, or by another reasonable means.

## 7. Who is the "employer" obligation on

The employer — not (only) the vendor. You can't outsource compliance. If you use a vendor's AEDT, you are responsible for:

- Ensuring a qualifying bias audit is in place
- Publishing the summary and dates on your website
- Providing notice to candidates

Work closely with your vendor to get the audit and to confirm distribution details, but the legal obligation sits with you.

## 8. Interaction with other laws

- **Title VII, ADA, ADEA** — federal anti-discrimination laws still apply
- **NYC Human Rights Law** — broader protected classes than federal law; violations can be independent of LL 144
- **EEOC guidance on AI in employment** — overlapping expectations
- **Colorado AI Act** — consequential employment decisions; overlap with CAIA obligations
- **Illinois AIVIA** — video interview analysis
- **California CCPA ADMT** — notice and opt-out for California candidates

## 9. Practical readiness steps

1. **Identify every tool** that might be an AEDT under the rule (resume screeners, video interview analyzers, online assessment platforms, sourcing tools with AI ranking, even some ATS features)
2. **Classify** each as AEDT or not, with a documented rationale
3. **Commission a bias audit** from a qualified independent auditor for every in-scope AEDT. Build this into your annual calendar.
4. **Publish** the audit summary and dates on the careers page. Make sure the link is findable.
5. **Draft and deploy candidate notice** in job postings and candidate communications. Give 10 business days before AEDT use.
6. **Establish alternative selection / accommodation process**. Train recruiters to handle these requests.
7. **Track distribution date** of each AEDT (when you first used it, with each version). Audit results are tied to distribution date.
8. **Retain data** supporting the bias audit and candidate interactions.
9. **Update vendor contracts** to require cooperation with bias audits and timely audit data.
10. **Train HR and recruiters** on what LL 144 requires.

## 10. Common traps

- **Assuming your ATS is not an AEDT.** Resume-screening features that score or rank candidates usually qualify.
- **Letting the vendor's audit stand in for yours.** A vendor-paid audit can be disqualified for independence; and the employer still must publish.
- **Burying the notice in a privacy policy.** Notice has specific content and a 10-business-day lead.
- **Forgetting intersectional categories.** The DCWP methodology requires sex × race/ethnicity breakdowns, not just marginals.
- **Not retaining the data.** Without data, you can't audit, and you can't defend the audit.
- **Using the tool before the audit.** Don't deploy a new AEDT or a materially modified version until the audit is current.
