# NYC LL 144 — Candidate Notice

Ready-to-adapt notice for candidates and employees when an Automated Employment Decision Tool (AEDT) will be used. Must be provided **at least 10 business days before** use of the AEDT.

Tailor to your specific AEDT, role, and process. Have employment counsel review before publishing.

---

## Option A — In the job posting

Include the following section in every NYC-based job posting and in postings open to NYC residents:

> **Use of Automated Employment Decision Tools**
>
> [COMPANY NAME] uses an Automated Employment Decision Tool (AEDT) as part of the selection process for this role.
>
> - **What it does:** [The AEDT scores resumes based on listed qualifications and relevant experience / Analyzes video interview responses / etc.]
> - **Job qualifications and characteristics used:** [LIST — e.g., years of experience with [TECH], demonstrated experience in [DOMAIN], alignment with job-related skills]
> - **Data collected and its source:** [LIST — e.g., information you provide in your resume and cover letter; your responses to a skills assessment; information from prior interactions if you have previously applied]
> - **Data retention:** We retain candidate data used by the AEDT for [RETENTION PERIOD] and delete it thereafter, subject to applicable law.
> - **Your rights:** You may request an accommodation for a disability or request an alternative selection process by emailing [CAREERS EMAIL] at least [3] business days before the AEDT is used. We will make reasonable efforts to accommodate your request.
>
> A summary of the most recent bias audit of this AEDT is available at [LINK].

## Option B — Standalone email to candidate (sent at application acknowledgment)

> **Subject:** About our hiring process for [POSITION TITLE]
>
> Hello [CANDIDATE NAME],
>
> Thank you for applying to the [POSITION TITLE] role at [COMPANY NAME]. We want to let you know — before any screening step — that our hiring process for this role uses an **Automated Employment Decision Tool (AEDT)**.
>
> **What the tool does.** [DESCRIBE IN 1-2 SENTENCES — e.g., "It reviews resumes and scores them based on the job qualifications listed in the posting."]
>
> **What it evaluates.** The AEDT considers [LIST — the job qualifications and characteristics used as inputs].
>
> **What data it uses.** The AEDT uses [LIST — the specific data: resume content, responses to application questions, etc.]. That data comes from [SOURCES — e.g., your application, your submitted resume, any assessments you complete].
>
> **How long we keep it.** We retain candidate data used by the AEDT for [RETENTION PERIOD], after which it is deleted, subject to legal obligations.
>
> **Your choices.**
>
> - You may request an **accommodation** for a disability — contact [CONTACT] with details. We'll work with you.
> - You may request an **alternative selection process** — we'll arrange for your application to be reviewed by a qualified human recruiter instead of the AEDT.
>
> To make either request, reply to this email or contact [CONTACT] at [EMAIL] **at least [3] business days before** [EVENT — e.g., "your scheduled assessment", "our application review window closes"].
>
> A summary of the most recent bias audit of this AEDT is available at [LINK].
>
> Thank you again for your interest,
>
> [RECRUITER / HR NAME]
> [COMPANY NAME]

## Option C — Employee promotion / internal-role notice

For promotions, use a variation — NYC LL 144 covers promotions as well as initial hiring. Replace "candidate" and "applicant" with "employee" and "considered for promotion."

> **Subject:** About the promotion process for [ROLE]
>
> Dear [EMPLOYEE NAME],
>
> You have been nominated for consideration for promotion to [ROLE]. As part of our selection process, we use an Automated Employment Decision Tool (AEDT). *[Continue with content from Option B, adapted for internal promotion context.]*

## Timing

- **10 business days minimum** before AEDT use. Post in job listings (which are open long enough to meet the window) and re-confirm in candidate communications.
- For rolling processes: make sure the window is preserved for each candidate individually.

## Accommodation and alternative process

You must be able to provide:

- An **accommodation** under ADA and state/local disability law (reasonable modification for disabilities)
- An **alternative selection process** that doesn't use the AEDT — typically a recruiter review

Train recruiters on how to handle both. Track requests and outcomes as part of your equal-employment records.

## Record-keeping

Retain:

- Copy of notice language (and version history)
- Timestamps of notice delivery
- Requests for accommodation / alternative process and outcomes
- Retention consistent with your employment and hiring records policy (typically 3–4 years minimum; check with employment counsel)
