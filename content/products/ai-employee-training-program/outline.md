# AI Employee Training Program — Outline

**Audience:** All employees and contractors with access to AI tools for work
**Duration:** ~30 minutes recorded (longer for live sessions with Q&A)
**Delivery:** Live session, recorded video, or LMS self-paced module
**Refresher cadence:** Annual, plus on material policy changes

> This program meets the spirit (and often the letter) of AI literacy obligations like **EU AI Act Art 4**, which requires that deployers take measures to ensure a sufficient level of AI literacy among staff. It also positions the organization to demonstrate "reasonable care" under Colorado AI Act and similar frameworks.

---

## Program objectives

By the end of this training, participants will be able to:

1. Describe what AI tools are approved for use at [COMPANY NAME] and what each is for
2. Apply the **green / yellow / red** data handling rules
3. Identify prohibited uses of AI
4. Recognize decisions that require human review
5. Disclose AI use to customers when required
6. Report concerns or suspected policy violations

## Module structure

| # | Module | Time | Format |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Why we're doing this | 3 min | Slides + context story |
| 2 | How AI helps us — and where it fails | 4 min | Slides + examples |
| 3 | Our policies and where to find them | 3 min | Slides + links |
| 4 | Approved tools and permitted uses | 4 min | Slides + live list |
| 5 | The green / yellow / red data rules | 4 min | Slides + scenarios |
| 6 | Prohibited uses | 3 min | Slides + examples |
| 7 | When a human must review | 3 min | Slides + scenarios |
| 8 | Disclosure and consent | 3 min | Slides + examples |
| 9 | Reporting and questions | 2 min | Slides + resources |
| 10 | Acknowledgment and quiz | 5 min | Self-paced |

## Role-specific add-ons

After the core 30 minutes, the following roles have an additional 10-15 minute module:

- **Engineers** — proprietary code rules, secrets-in-prompts, code-assist etiquette
- **Marketing** — AI-drafted content disclosure, brand voice, copyright basics
- **Sales** — bot disclosure, CRM hygiene, outreach rules
- **HR** — hiring AI rules, AEDT notice and audit, accommodation process
- **Customer support** — chatbot handoff, confidentiality, accurate information
- **Finance** — financial-data handling, model outputs as inputs to decisions, fraud detection oversight
- **Leadership** — governance responsibilities, incident escalation, board reporting

The core module is mandatory; role modules are assigned based on job function.

## Prerequisites and follow-ups

- Prerequisite: none
- Follow-ups:
  - Read the AI Acceptable Use Policy
  - Sign the AI Acceptable Use Policy Acknowledgment
  - Join the [#ai-questions] channel
  - Subscribe to [COMPANY NAME]'s internal AI updates newsletter
- Role-specific follow-ups listed in the role module outlines

## Success criteria

- 95%+ completion within [14 days] of assignment
- 80%+ quiz pass rate on first attempt
- 100% acknowledgment on file before AI tool access is granted

## Maintenance

- Refresh content whenever the policy suite is updated
- Re-record or re-deliver at least annually
- Keep examples current — AI tools and regulatory landscape change quickly
